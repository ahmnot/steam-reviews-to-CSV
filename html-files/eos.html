Night n Day.

Story:

You play as Eos. You travel to the island where your mother used to live tasked with granting her final wish, rest her medallion on the island, but once you step on the island you find yourself followed by a voice that knows quite a lot about you.

Since the game is barely ten minutes long, that's all I'll really say about the story. It's a decent enough story and has a quite nice set up and conclusion. It's not original by any means, but it works well enough to set up the journey you'll have on the island.

Graphics:

Now I haven't mentioned this before, but this whole game is a student project, so it's very simplistic in it's design in pretty much every category. Visually it looks pretty nice, the colors are vibrant and the night and day contrast although very simple does look pretty good.

Although there are issues with the visuals. There's tons of texture popping and often then not the bricks on the ground will flicker which makes it hard to look at from time to time. Also whenever you look up at the sky you can see borders which makes it seem like you're in a giant cube.

Audio:

Since this is a student project I can't really say I expected good voice acting, but it could have been way worse. I've played many paid games with acting way worse than this, but what I will say is that the audio quality is horrible. The actress of Eos very clearly used different microphones to record her lines. Some sound crystal clear, some sound like she recorded them through a wall using her phone.

I'm pretty certain the music is royalty free, but it's pretty nice for background noise. It's not really intrusive and has a calm and relaxing tone to match it's visuals. There are issues with the music cutting out from time to time, but it's not that big of an issue, cause it loops back from the start if it cuts out.

Gameplay:

Eos is a very simplistic puzzle game where the main gimmick of the game is that you use a night and day cycle to finish puzzles, but calling them puzzles is a very generous way of describing it. You basically walk through portals, where one time of day unlocks a door which has an item you need and then you flip to deliver that item to the place that needs it.

There will be no brain scratching here. It's all very straight forward, but it's actually quite fun. I could totally see myself playing a fully fleshed out version of this type of puzzle game with more complex puzzles, but what's here won't take you longer than 10 minutes to finish all of it.

Verdict:

Eos is a great little prototype for an adventure game. Since this is a student project there's definitely a few issues, but as far it goes, there's worse ways to spend 10 minutes and since this is a free game I can definitely recommend this for a nice little game.

Final Rating:
7.5/10
