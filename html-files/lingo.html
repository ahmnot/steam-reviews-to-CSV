Reviews will tell you that Lingo is a cross between a word puzzle game, The Witness, and Antichamber. Often in those old titles, the puzzles use insane moon logic, and the game becomes less about understanding the rules of the game and more about trying to intuit the developer's logic and guess what they intended.

As a made-up example which is very similar to actual puzzles in the game, imagine you have the word "crash" and you are trying to guess a five-letter word which replaces one sound from that word with another one. Crash only has one syllable. You may now guess literally any single-syllable, five-letter word in the English language and, by the game's rules, it would be valid. There are dozens of instances in the discord of people who just pull down lists of English words and brute force their way through puzzles like this, because sometimes, this is the kind of BS the game will throw at you.

As it might turn out, the developer considered "crash" to have two sounds, crr-ash, if you enunciate it weirdly, and so considers "brash" to be the correct answer. Maybe the puzzle is multilayered, and it's in a room where all of the answers are synonyms to brave/bold, and so "brash" is actually a very clever answer. More than once, I felt absolutely stupid, thinking the developer was just a mean idiot, when it was it was I that whole time. Maybe the word "brash" shows up in lots of puzzles, and the developer has effectively primed you to jump to that word when you see anything that might be it.

These are the kinds of Myst/Monkey Island puzzles you might deal with in Lingo, and it's a little bit brilliant, a little bit frustrating, and a little bit insane. When you're solving puzzles elsewhere and they're telling you "brash" you do not know that you're picking up an item you can use in a later puzzle, and for me, that was a step too far into frustration. This game was not for me, and I think for most people, it will not be for them either.

That criticism aside, this game just has so much love and content in it. Even if I frequently became stuck or didn't enjoy some sets of puzzles -- or worse, spent most of my time wandering around lost instead of solving a puzzle at all -- you'll note that I still got all the achievements and put a big chunk of hours in. I always wanted to see what else the game had in store. The ideas on display here are brilliant, even if I don't think they are conveyed well or mercifully, and that's the kind of stuff I buy and play indie games for. There are moments of true, absolute brilliance hidden in the maze of this game, and I delighted in finding them.

At the core of it, it contains a lot of word puzzles in the form of a hint word and a space to fill in the answer word based on the hint. There is a reasonable process of teaching you how to associate between these visual clues and what rule set to apply, but the game does not hold your hand at all. The other aspect of the grand puzzle is navigating the world, or what you might also call exploration. 由于我太菜了，所以准备搁置这个游戏，但就我玩过的部分来看，这个游戏的想法和创意确实很有意思，所以还是打算写个小评测推荐给大家。

《Lingo》是一个以英文单词为核心的第一人称解谜游戏。这款游戏让我产生了许多的既视感，最强烈的包括《见证者》和《Antichamber》，至于为什么，看我下面的介绍你就明白了。

本作的主要玩法是解决散布在场景中的题板，而每个题板的答案，都是一个英语单词，或者少数情况下，是词组。不过英语单词这么多，什么样的单词才是“正确”的呢？你会注意到，每个题板上都有一个提示词（也有可能是词组，但下面还是只说“提示词”），正确的答案词一定和这个提示词有关系。那到底是什么关系呢？对于不同的题板，答案可能很不一样。

不过本作最有意思的地方就在这里：它依照某种统一的规律，在某些地方告诉了玩家提示词和答案之间的关系，或者换句话来说，“限制”了提示词和答案之间的关系，但玩家需要自己思考和猜测游戏在什么地方告诉了玩家这一点，背后又遵照了什么样的规律。没错，到这你就会发现，并不是只有“第一人称”和“题板”这两点让我想到《见证者》，在让玩家“猜规则”的方面，本作也有一些《见证者》的味道。

再说多了就剧透了，但估计你看到这里还是觉得一头雾水。不剧透的话，我只能再提示一点：“Lingo”是“行话”的意思，不过好像还有“语言特征”的意思，本作所提示的“关系”，就和各种各样的语言特征有关。至于游戏给玩家提示这种关系的方法，就和《见证者》差不多，使用某种“符号化”的，抽象的方式给予玩家提示。如果你实在想知道，或者你已经玩过了游戏，但还是迷迷糊糊，下面是我自己理解的一些东西。

关于游戏会提示什么样的关系：本作感觉主要关注三种语言特征：语义、拼写和发音。提示词和答案在这些语言特征上，可以有“相同”、“相反”、“部分和整体”等等这样的关系。组合在一起就是真正要提示的关系，比如“语义相同”=“近义词”，“拼写相反”=“倒着写”等等。

关于游戏怎么提示这样的关系：本作用题板背后方块的颜色和高低位置来提示上面所说的关系，不同的颜色和高低位置对应着不同的“关系组分”，将所有组分合在一起才能知道题目到底想表达什么。

上面说的部分还算是本作比较基本的部分，是提示单个关系的方法。在游戏更后期，前面所说的东西也会继续延伸、组合，形成更加复杂的提示或限制。还有的时候，本作的谜题也会像《见证者》一样，和环境联系在一起。

总而言之，当玩家完全理解了游戏提示关系的方法之后，本作的解谜就成了“字谜”，也就是找到和提示词有特定关系的另一个词。到了这个地步，我觉得考的就是词汇量和脑洞，而非逻辑了。如果你常玩各种各样的猜词游戏，可能会比较熟练，不过我是不太行了，很多时候依赖在线词典和各种查词网站的帮助才能解题，甚至这样都还解不出来。我可能还是更擅长逻辑方面的解谜，这是我决定搁置本作的原因之一。

让我决定搁置本作的另一个原因就是本作迷宫一样的地图设计。不仅和迷宫一样绕来绕去，缺乏显眼的地标，甚至还有《Antichamber》式的“不可能空间”，这就使得本作的地图基本不能用“常理”理解，寻找和记忆路径异常困难，至少对我来说是这样。就算是《Antichamber》，里面也有类似“路线图”一样的东西辅助玩家记忆和规划路线，还有便捷的快速传送系统，而本作虽然有后者，但不得不说，真的不如《Antichamber》做得好。

综上所述，我认为本作是有很有意思的创意和想法的，但是具体到游戏体验，只能说不太适合我。推荐给大家。

I'm a regular of the puzzle genre of all types and usually binge-purchase a bunch of puzzle games at once and make my way through them before buying some more. Not hearing much about lingo but just letting the steam tag system inform me of it's existence, and just looking very briefly at the description and screenshots (and honestly, the name) - I was expecting it to be one of those 2-4 hour little games that eat up an evening where it may have a decent idea but is just poorly executed or not explored enough (though not mutually exclusive).

I was completely surprised to find that the game blew away my expectations. Don't get me wrong; it has it's faults, but it's very refreshing to find a game that hits the design aspect just right. For me, puzzle games that hit the spot are ones that introduce new mechanics specifically for the *right* amount of time. They aren't 'there and then immediately gone' or 'overstay their welcome that they become a chore'. You get this baseline just right and i will enjoy ever ounce of the game, and will then enjoy any twists and turns. This game does exactly that.

You will see many mentions of The witness, Antichamber and such and they're not wrong about that, it's a very nice mixture that wasn't exactly necessary, but is done in good taste.

One unique aspect of this game that you may not expect is it works fantastically as a multiplayer experience. Both the textual puzzles AND the navigation of the world both work towards this, even if only a single player/streamer uses the mouse and keyboard.

The textual puzzles are akin to most of the word games out there, you'll feel right at home if you enjoy scrabble, words with friends, wordle, etc.

As for it's faults; some answers can feel like stretches. There is no hint system nor guidance. The first 75% of the game is a lot of fun as even if you get stuck there is always something else to go and do, but the further you get, the frustrations do tend to brew when you're having to deal with just finding the next area. Several times I got completely stuck just simply trying to find more puzzles and the answer to find such areas came down to complete brute force.

Imagine you start a game and there's 2 doors in front of you, and every time you open a door there's 2 more, but behind each door there are new puzzles, things will feel great and fresh almost always. But lets say you now have 200 doors unlocked and you know there are more puzzles. How do you find it? Well obviously you missed checking 1 of those 200 doors somewhere, so you have to start exploring each door again to find whichever door you missed. Oh but, there's 200 doors just on this floor! Perhaps there's 5 floors with 200 each. You have no guidance, no in-game progression, no heads up at all on where you need to start looking in any floor.

This is a major aspect of the game that I feel many players may become frustrated at. The core puzzles are this brand new and unique mechanic that no other puzzle game has done and they are very addicting, and when you get to a point where this addiction is interrupted by an hour of exploring an ENORMOUS non-echelon maze, it can be quite demoralising.

A huge part of enjoyment of this game comes from the community around it itself. I highly recommend joining the discord, and even too I would recommend streaming the game if you can, there are several members of the discord who like to watch/join in and it makes these exploration moments much less tedious.

Also don't shy away from using some online word based tools in moments where you feel like you need help, this game can venture into the English Language quite extremely at times. A pen and paper go a long way in this game.

I've played my fair share of puzzle games over the year- and the sheer majority without any sort of hints or guides (including baba is you 100%), yet even in this game I felt and relied on online word tools at times.
Including this knowledge, as well as streaming to viewers who had 100% freedom to help me with figuring out word puzzles and such, it still took me just under 20 hours to fully complete, which is far more content than I ever would have hoped for.

Enjoyed all moments in the game, even those frustrating moments, and the community around it are a lot of fun to talk too.

Highly recommend! So much so I even nominated it as part of my steam game award nominations.

Well done ! :)
