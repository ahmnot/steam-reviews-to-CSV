TL;DR A solid, but very niche, hidden gem. Fully recommended if it piques your interest

And with that said here's my attempt at a more lenghty review for however needs it.

While arguably a little barebones in terms of actual content, if you're the type like me to loose yourself in automation-type games like Factorio and Satisfactory, to name some examples, this deffinetely hits the spot, as most of your sense of accomplishment is figuring out your own way to connect everything into one cohesive network (and in my case, also figuring out how to make said network look nice)

The underground is however by no means entirely free of critters that are less than receptive to your presence, even if you are just the IT guy. So that slight nagging sense of dread in the back of your head of "just what might I run into this time", once you start branching out beyond your starting area, is to me what turns this game into a real hidden gem of an experience, as the exploration tied with the feel of actual danger once you encounter your first hostile critter, does quite a bit of preventing the work of perfecting the network from feeling dull.

If I were to offer a gripe or complaint, it would be the map that you are provided, as while the gameworld is in a 3D space, the map is on a single 2D plane and also cannot be zoomed, unless it is a function that I myself have yet to unlock (as more and more equipment/upgrades open up to you as you progress in your task). Which takes quite a bit of getting used to

While I wouldnt call it a bug free experience either, the deveveloper has been exceptionally responsive and quick indeed to try and iron out any issues presented. Which alone is more than enough for me to wanna give this game my full support.

If this game piques your interest, I couldnt recommend it more, especially considering the pricepoint and extraordinarely quick support from the developer. Just do not expect AAA quality gameplay. This game knows it is niche and makes no attempt to hide it either

This is a very special game. This game is interesting, intricate, and technical despite a simple set of tools. The atmosphere is great, and it generally feels satisfying to implement a solution to any individual part of your network's needs. From a story perspective, the game lets you (literally) dig up the lore for yourself and slowly uncover the Tunnet and its residents. I recommend this game wholeheartedly.
