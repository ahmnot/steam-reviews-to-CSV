For starters, I'll say I'm used to First Person Puzzle games, I like this genre. I completed Portal, Talos Principle, Magnetic Cage Closed, QUBE, Antichamber, and others, including ReThink (the previous game).


Now, the review:
ReThink | Evolved is, basically, a direct improvement of ReThink, as the name suggested it.

Core mechanic: you place various cubes freely (instead of on a grid-like structure for the previous game) to redirect colored lasers from their source to colored end points. Several types of cubes are there, from the basic "straight that way" to "split the colors", "source cubes" and a few others. In later tests, "shieldbridges" (toggleable bridges) will add some difficulty. Later again, you'll have to think the puzzles in 3D.
If you didn't like the floor-tiles puzzles from the first game, there are still a few in this one, but waaaaay less.
About the plot, "you have to do puzzles for some reason". Don't expect more than that. Not a problem, this game doesn't pretend to do more.

Now, pros and ♥♥♥♥ (at the time I'm writing this):
Pros:
- Solid core mechanic with various elements, keeps it interesting to the end.
- Annoying mechanics from the previous game are either removed or improved.
- quite long game, and sometimes you have to choose your path (two different sets of puzzles, happens 3 times). Expect 3 to 6 hours to complete it the first time, based on your experience with FPP games.
- Smooth difficulty curve.
- A working fidget spinner. Because reasons.
- Beautiful graphics.
- Beautiful non intrusive ambient music and sounds.
- Several options, especially for an indie game. Amongst others: FOV slider, detailed performance settings, rebindable keys.
- Currently available in 4 languages, only through texts (English, German, French and Swedish).
- Active developper, will fix small bugs within hours at most.

♥♥♥♥:
- The fidget spinner falls on the ground when you take a cube, so you might lose it if you're not careful.
- No real plot, but that's not a real problem.

- Compared to Portal:

- Compared to Talos Principle:
- Blue and red lasers: in Talos, lasers collide. They. Collide. If you still think this game is a clone, I feel sorry for you.

Évaluation publiée le 2 mars 2018
ReThink Evolved is a first person puzzle game similar to Portal and The Talos Principle. The player must progress through a series of test chambers, where the primary puzzle mechanic involves using cubes to redirect coloured laser beams to activate switches of the same colour.

The basic concept is easy to understand, but later levels add complexity by including other objects and hazards.
- Use “V” or “W” cubes to send a single beam in multiple directions, to simultaneously activate two or three switches.
- Combine red, blue and green lasers to activate purple, yellow, turquoise and white switches.
- Use special cubes to split purple, yellow, turquoise and white lasers back into red, blue and green.
- Weigh down pressure plates to deactivate cube destroying barriers.
- Activate light bridges, float through pockets of low gravity, and use bounce pads to cross gaps and reach higher platforms.
- Use remote control cubes to toggle switches on and off, for example to lower a lift, then raise it again after you’ve stepped on.

This is the second ReThink game, and the most obvious difference compared to the first game is that cubes are no longer restricted to specific floor tiles. You have freedom to position cubes almost anywhere, which means that some puzzles could have multiple valid solutions. In fact there were a couple of puzzles where my solution didn’t even use all the available cubes.

My biggest complaint is that the low gravity areas were kind of tedious. There were times when I knew what I wanted to do, but I had difficulty positioning a cube in just the right place to activate multiple switches – especially as these sections require you to consider the cube’s height off the ground as well as angle the laser beams up or down.

The game also ends quite anticlimactically. After you solve the last puzzle it just kind of fades to black and dumps you back at the main menu. There’s no story, so there’s nothing left unresolved, but even so I would have expected ... something. You don’t even get an achievement.

Technical performance is fine. I didn’t suffer any bugs or crashes, no framerate problems, controls are fully rebindable and work well with mouse and keyboard.

It only took me around 4.5 hours to solve all the puzzles, but the purchase price is cheap, so I’d recommend picking this up at full price if you like puzzle games.

Évaluation publiée le 9 aout 2020
While this is a sequel of sorts the ReThink with the same puzzle mechanism (laser light manipulation), Evolved is different in that there are no nodes, but rather cubes you can place free-form. Cubes can be placed anywhere you can reach and at any angle. While this makes the challenge slightly higher, the puzzle layout is done in a way that you can generally rule out cube placements.

Evolved also adds one new cube to the mix, a switch cube. When you place this cube the light goes in one direction. Press your middle mouse button and the switch is activated changing the direction of the light. This was a clever mechanic that allowed for you to remotely trigger switches to activate platforms or deactivate barrier walls. The last puzzle in each path used them for a multiple solution puzzle to open two sets of doors in sequence.

There are three sets of puzzles - grey (neutral), orange, and blue. at various points in the game you select a path to take - orange or blue. After five or six puzzles you return to the gray and then to another fork. It is a unique way to play through the game a second time.

With the free-form placement, Evolved also introduces 3D puzzle manipulation. In the later levels, there are blue anti-gravity zones where you can place cubes at any height and at any angle. Again, this may seem like an infinite challenge, but the puzzles never become too much.

There are a couple of nit-pics. With the free-form placement, it can sometimes be a pain to line up the cubes right. When you move or turn, you move a minimum amount and sometimes that was just too much to line up right. It becomes a dance to line up all the cubes to light up the exit lights. There are also a couple of rooms where you have to light up lights on a grid. You can't cross the red line or step on the same light twice. Puzzle 41 blue uses this to open a door and puts a unique twist I won't spoil it. While I applaud the developer trying something new, these are not my cup of tea.

I did like Evolved more than the original. I don't know if this was due to the free-form placement, the new cubes, the different puzzle designs, or just that it was a sophomore game. In my mind, this felt more put together. I will be looking at the other games in this series as each version adds new mechanics.

Thank you to the developer and publisher of Rethink Evolved, Yaeko, for making a really good puzzle game that let me escape, think and most importantly enjoy.

I never heard of Rethink Evolved until I saw it in one of the “similar” lists on a game i recently completed. I play a lot of puzzle games and indie titles, how did i miss this one? This game is fantastic in every way from performance, puzzles and graphics it is easily a must play for anyone who wants to scratch that puzzle fix.

Rethink Evolved is pretty bare bones and does not differ from most other puzzle story plot lines. You have volunteered(where do they find all these people?) to solve puzzles, that's pretty much the story. There is no voice acting and only brief messages you receive on a iphone like device. There is a message at the end that hints at continued updates/DLC as the end room showed lots of doors with no access...yet.

One of the things that is apparent at the start of Rethink is the beautiful environments and color in the game. Glass ceilings show bright blue skies, straight lines and clean environments highlight a modern architecture design. The use of green trees inside against an orange and white backdrop really makes it shine and the multiple colored lasers add to an already beautiful palette. There is a lot of graphics settings to choose and console commands are also available. I played the game with everything on high settings(there is an epic choice) and was getting 35-45 frames pretty consistent but it felt so much faster and I never had any issues.

The puzzles in Rethink will have you for the most part directing multiple colored laser light beams to a given point on a wall that will open the door to the next area. To manipulate the colored beams you will use cubes that will either shoot one beam, split a beam into two beams or one cube will even split into 3 colors (red, blue, green). Its pretty instinctive especially if you have done maybe some other color focused puzzle games prior. There are also cubes that will pull on objects such as platforms and there are elevators, pressure plates, force fields and static cubes to keep things interesting. One new mechanic(if its a mechanic) is an area in later rooms that will be encased in a somewhat no atmosphere/ zero gravity field. Here you are free to float and place cubes to direct beams. Its super easy to navigate and really cool when you start seeing multiple cubes just floating and directing the light. There are also a few puzzles, maybe to just give a break, that seem completely out of place. Think Saturday night fever as you walk around lighting up the floor in sequence to simply open the door.

Unlike most puzzle games of this nature where you solve a puzzle, exit and enter a new room, Rethink separates some of puzzle rooms with glass. Basically, you can see the puzzle you just completed while working on the new one. There's actually a puzzle where you are above the previous puzzle and have to direct light overtop of the old. Yes, it does not have anything to do with gameplay. Its just a unique, cool visual idea that i dont think i ever have seen before and seeing the colored lasers in a completed state is really cool.

I like Rethink evolved a lot but i dont actually buy their ‘freedom’ to solve the puzzles pitch. I think this is very untrue especially in the later puzzles. Yes, maybe the freedom is where you place a certain block in a few puzzles but because you have limited blocks some of them HAVE to be placed in an exact spot to make the puzzle work. Did i buy the game for this reason, no, but wanted to make a point I do not agree with their “freedom” marketing.

Rethink Evolved feels fresh what has become a growing and crowded game genre. I really enjoy and have played a lot of puzzle games and this title has some really cool, unique ideas and the color pallette is beautiful. I hope it will continue to receive support in the future as it’s an easy recommendation to anyone looking for their next puzzle challenge.

Notes
Played High settings 1080P(ultrawide) 35-45 frames MSI R9/390

Positives

Performance- lots of graphic options
Good, fair, fun challenging puzzles
Beautiful environments
Possible free expansions incoming
Feels fresh

Negatives

‘Freedom’ marketing
No story
Floor puzzles feel out of place

A little nice indie puzzle game with lasers !
Not a big AAA game for sure, but it has a well made game design !

41 levels in which you have to match lasers colors to their corresponding receptors.
For that, you have a few differents cube types :
• Redirect cube : to redirect the light beam to a particular point
• Emitter : emit a light beam and you can choose the color
• Reverse redirect : same as the basic redirect cube except that you can toggle the light beam to go in front on the cube or behind it
• Dividers to divide a light beam into 2 or 3 beams of the same color
• Refracter : decompose a composite light beam into its primary elements (green, red, blue)

Have fun ! ;)

TL:DR If you enjoy first person puzzle games you'll probably enjoy this game - and the rest of the series.

This is a beautifully rendered logic game that fans of Portal will enjoy for the chance to solve more button and cube tests within a pristine futuristic architecture but with the clever addition of light puzzles - reflection, redirection, addition & subtraction of colors. You are in a building made of glass and panels, you can see the outside world with busy aircars bustling about but until you solve the puzzles, you are on your own - no GladOS, Wheatley or announcer, and no real chance of death. The music is very relaxing and will stay with you, and the architecture is so beautiful that I often just stand in various spots and enjoy it (there's the pity that it can't be visited!). The Evolved series differs from the other ReThink series in that you have full ability to place and point cubes anywhere rather than just at designated spots.
The entire Rethink and ReThink | Evolved series of games are very enjoyable - clever and engaging puzzles of increasing difficulty and more and more creative elements as the series progresses.

IMPORTANT: One key point is that you will likely have to tinker with the graphics settings to find a combination of settings that will work for your machine. Stick with it - I was originally not able to play the first installments of either series until I went back and set all settings to lowest values and experimented with incrementing them.
