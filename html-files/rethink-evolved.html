While this is a sequel of sorts the ReThink with the same puzzle mechanism (laser light manipulation), Evolved is different in that there are no nodes, but rather cubes you can place free-form. Cubes can be placed anywhere you can reach and at any angle. While this makes the challenge slightly higher, the puzzle layout is done in a way that you can generally rule out cube placements.

Evolved also adds one new cube to the mix, a switch cube. When you place this cube the light goes in one direction. Press your middle mouse button and the switch is activated changing the direction of the light. This was a clever mechanic that allowed for you to remotely trigger switches to activate platforms or deactivate barrier walls. The last puzzle in each path used them for a multiple solution puzzle to open two sets of doors in sequence.

There are three sets of puzzles - grey (neutral), orange, and blue. at various points in the game you select a path to take - orange or blue. After five or six puzzles you return to the gray and then to another fork. It is a unique way to play through the game a second time.

With the free-form placement, Evolved also introduces 3D puzzle manipulation. In the later levels, there are blue anti-gravity zones where you can place cubes at any height and at any angle. Again, this may seem like an infinite challenge, but the puzzles never become too much.

There are a couple of nit-pics. With the free-form placement, it can sometimes be a pain to line up the cubes right. When you move or turn, you move a minimum amount and sometimes that was just too much to line up right. It becomes a dance to line up all the cubes to light up the exit lights. There are also a couple of rooms where you have to light up lights on a grid. You can't cross the red line or step on the same light twice. Puzzle 41 blue uses this to open a door and puts a unique twist I won't spoil it. While I applaud the developer trying something new, these are not my cup of tea.

I did like Evolved more than the original. I don't know if this was due to the free-form placement, the new cubes, the different puzzle designs, or just that it was a sophomore game. In my mind, this felt more put together. I will be looking at the other games in this series as each version adds new mechanics.

This is a beautifully rendered logic game that fans of Portal will enjoy for the chance to solve more button and cube tests within a pristine futuristic architecture but with the clever addition of light puzzles - reflection, redirection, addition & subtraction of colors. You are in a building made of glass and panels, you can see the outside world with busy aircars bustling about but until you solve the puzzles, you are on your own - no GladOS, Wheatley or announcer, and no real chance of death. The music is very relaxing and will stay with you, and the architecture is so beautiful that I often just stand in various spots and enjoy it (there's the pity that it can't be visited!). The Evolved series differs from the other ReThink series in that you have full ability to place and point cubes anywhere rather than just at designated spots.
The entire Rethink and ReThink | Evolved series of games are very enjoyable - clever and engaging puzzles of increasing difficulty and more and more creative elements as the series progresses.

IMPORTANT: One key point is that you will likely have to tinker with the graphics settings to find a combination of settings that will work for your machine. Stick with it - I was originally not able to play the first installments of either series until I went back and set all settings to lowest values and experimented with incrementing them.
