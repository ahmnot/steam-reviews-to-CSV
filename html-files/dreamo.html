DREAMO is a narrative puzzle walking simulator that has quite fun puzzles to solve but with a story that felt flat in the end.

Gameplay
Using a polygon graphics art style, the game gives some The Witness vibe but the gameplay is linear. We can only solve one puzzle after the other, and except for the beginning area of the game, we mostly walk forward while listening to the story.

Puzzles
I personally found the puzzles the best part of the game. Given a limited number of specific gear types, they consist of finding the correct gears wiring to an exit gear. Difficulty is perfectly balanced through the game as it gradually increases as we progress and more mechanics are introduced.
The game also offers a challenge mode with 10 extra puzzles (the first few are in the demo) with a short story that might have happened before the main game story.

Plot
We play as Jack who is in a coma after a plane crash. Thanks to some very advanced medical technology, a doctor, Tara, can communicate with him through his dreams, helping him to fix his brain damage (by solving puzzles), recover his memories, and maybe wake up?
The story is told after activating some dialogue icon found along the way. We can then listen to (or read) a dialogue between Jack and Tara before walking to the next one or puzzle. This aspect might annoy some impatient player as we have to stay next to the dialogue if we want to hear/read it.
Also, I would say the story can be divided in 4 parts, most of it was quite interesting to listen to, but the last part and ending were confusing as many questions that were raised during the game were not answered.

Needs for improvement
- The game only saves after solving a puzzle, so if we quit beforehand, we will have to listen again to all dialogues since the last save.
- There is a slight up and down head bobbing while walking. I admit that it didn't bother me, as I only noticed it after I saw a thread in the forum about it, but as of now, there is no option to remove it.


In all, even if the story felt lacking, I quite enjoyed the puzzle aspect of DREAMO. Some of the latest puzzles (story and challenge modes) were hard, but nothing impossible to figure out after taking a break. I would definitely recommend this game just for the puzzles.

DREAMO
梦想（猜测标题意大利语），一款由Hypnotic Ants制作而成、Carbon Studio发行的以故事驱动进行冒险解谜的游戏。游戏讲述了药理学家杰克虽然在飞机失事中死里逃生，不过需要在梦境中找回并连接自己的记忆，努力恢复意识...是现实还是梦境，如何从梦境中走出来，围绕记忆和选择展开游戏的主题，在沉醉的交互声音与对话下，解锁神秘的剧情故事，通过破解神器找寻最终的记忆。全语音叙事加上引人入胜的剧情以及量大管饱的谜题汇成这个多彩的梦境世界~
拥抱悲伤·前期剧情
你遇到飞机失事、你的父亲是个混蛋、你的母亲患上了绝症、远离交际的你没有朋友、你研究的药物间接害死了许多的人、帮你走出梦境的女医生是真的想拯救你吗？
杰克·温斯洛，一位著名的药理学家，在一次飞机失事中死里逃生，然后陷入昏迷。科技的发展，让杰克进入了梦境世界，在塔拉·莫罗医生的外部协助下，杰克的任务是找到并连接你的记忆，努力恢复意识；随着梦境的延伸，温馨的海滩眨眼破碎，杰克发现自己的部分梦境似乎是别人操控下的谎言，事情的真相到底是什么，杰克在飞机失事前到底有着怎样的故事......
这是款很酷的游戏
1.不断延伸的梦境和颇具挑战性的三维立方体谜题
杰克的记忆被锁在神器中，它们在杰克的脑海中显现为三维立方体，上面带有错位的齿轮。随着梦境的演变，立方体的内部机制变得越来越复杂，利用地上的齿轮，破解立方体，获得回忆并以此延伸自己的梦境世界。
游戏的立方体机制是解密的核心部分，游戏多达几十个独立设计且颇具挑战性的三维立方体谜题。随着梦境的扩展，通过解密时的喃喃自语来教会你立方体的运作方式，利用移动轨道、重力轨道、激光、纹路、翻转面壁、反转世界等等来加大游戏难度，为玩家献上精彩的烧脑体验！
2.一个愿望，两份满足~不仅仅谜题，还有悬疑故事
游戏分为故事模式和挑战模式，俩者的解密各不相同。通过挑战模式提升自身的破解机能；通过剧情模式，沉浸于一位药理学家的人生抉择。
3.多彩的低多边形游戏环境+全语音舒适表演。
游戏采取对话来推动游戏的展开，对话有俏皮、愤怒等等情绪，配合文本游戏表现相当不错；男女音色不错，不可能给你英语听力一样让人昏昏入睡的播报体验。
而多彩的游戏世界宣传动画表现得比我写的更好。

It's too bad that there isn't a 'maybe' option for recommendations, because that is about where my feelings lie with this game.

On the upside, most of the game is very engaging. I like the visuals, and I like that we slowly work our way back in the main character's mind to figure out what's going on. However, that all goes out the window in the final chapter, and for me the only reason for that is the ridiculously difficult final puzzle. I normally save my reviews for once I've beaten a game, but I honestly don't know if I can beat the game. I consider myself decently good at puzzle games, but the puzzle itself suddenly becomes so convoluted that my options are to spend an exorbitant amount of time to beat the puzzle or watch a video of someone else doing it and try to copy their movements (and I'll be honest it's so finicky that even while doing the latter I'm struggling). Narratively, I admit the puzzle being that way makes sense, but it is beyond frustrating nonetheless.

Overall, I would still recommend this game because 90% of it is good, but be warned that the final chapter is more of a roadblock than a payoff.
