It isn't without its technical and mechanical problems. There are a few bugs. I notably soft-locked myself three times, either entirely falling off the map, falling off an elevator that would never come back down, or getting stuck between some kind of mountains (thankfully there's a "restart level" option). Then what you are expected to do is often far from clear, and both the fact that solutions don't always make sense and the fact that the game is inconsistent when it comes to detecting when you are pointing an important object and thus letting you interact with it can make things much worse.

There are also long and repetitive sequences. In a whole, it is likely most players will feel like the game is overstaying its welcome. And both the puzzles and "action" sequences get more and more difficult and weird to a point where it's genuinely frustrating.

Yet, regardless of those "shortcomings", I'd somehow still recommend the game. In truth, I feel like this game wasn't meant to be fun. It's a mentally challenging experience that keeps being engaging and repulsive, visually appealing and ugly, audibly interesting and annoying, all at the same time. There's something that strangely kept pushing me through it even though at times I thought the game was terrible. In the end, I feel like finishing the game is not unlike waking up from a nightmare, though one that leaves a reflexive mark instead of a painful one.

All in all, while I recommend the game, note that this is clearly not for everyone. It's a dark, difficult and confusing experience that needs serious effort to go through.
