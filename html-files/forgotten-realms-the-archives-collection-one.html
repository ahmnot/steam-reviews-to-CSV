There seems to be three basic types of CRPG out there, you have the solo hero that does it all (Fallout, UnderRail), the ragtag group of four to six heroes who work together, have internal conflicts, and get a large story out of (Baldur's Gate, Divinity: Original Sin II), and there are the blobbers, which consists of a group of (usually) four party members in a box formation moving through a grid-based world and almost (not always as in Legend of Grimrock II) entirely set within a dungeon. That's what Eye of the Beholder is, a classic blobber set in the Advanced Dungeons & Dragons (the THAC0 system!) ruleset and it's entirely under the city of Waterdeep.

You and your other three party members are on a quest to kill a BBEG, that's all you need to know. You go down and kill everything in between and solve the puzzles while you're there because that's the other major aspect of the game, solving puzzles to descend further.

I've played my share of blobbers, and the first Eye is well worth the price of $10 and you'll get your moneys worth. The old school blobbers like this, you had to physically make a map on gridpaper and there are tricks within the dungeons (teleporting squares, spinner squares, switches to open a door on the other side of the level) that would make physical mapping much more difficult. Secondly, if it's that much of an issue, there's a dang option to turn the addon off in the start.

FAZIT

Die Eye of the Beholder Triologie von Westwood und SSI stammt aus den Jahren 1991-1993, war überaus populär und hat Rollenspiele insgesamt zugänglicher gemacht. Es handelt sich dabei um Oldschool Grid-Based Dungeon-Crawler, die zusammen mit dem legendären Dungeon Master meine ersten Spielerfahrungen in diesem Genre darstellten und die mich bis heute geprägt haben.
Die Stadt Tiefwasser in den Vergessenen Reichen wird von Monstern der Unterwelt insbesondere der Kanalisation heimgesucht. Die Fürsten der Stadt haben daraufhin beschlossen Helden anzuheuern.
Man startet mit vier Charakteren auf Stufe 3 (EoB I), bzw. Stufe 6 (EoB II), bzw. Stufe 11 (EoB III) die man dann sogar in die nächsten Abenteuer mitnehmen kann.
Gekämpft wird in nicht pausierbarer Echtzeit auf 12 Ebenen gegen diverse unterschiedliche Monster. In EoB II-III gibt es dann sogar Außenareale und voneinander getrennte Dungeons.
Das Magiesystem beruht auf über Nacht eingeprägten bzw. erbetenen Zaubern und Nahrung muss auch regelmäßig eingenommen werden (später kann diese aber vom Kleriker auch magisch erschaffen werden).
EoB II reduziert die Ganeplay-Schwächen des 1. Teils. Die Levelgrenzen und die Komplexität der Karten wurden reduziert, Teleporter und Wirbelfallen, die die Orientierung erschweren, werden in geringerem Maße eingesetzt. Die Erkundung wird dadurch geradliniger. Rollenspielaspekte, wie z.B. die Dialogoptionen, mehr Text und Rätsel ausgebaut und die Zwischensequenzen mit animierten Grafiken gestaltet. Auch die Bandbreite an Monstern wird größer.
EoB III lässt von der Qualität wieder etwas nach, ist aber dennoch gut.

TL; DR the Eye of the Beholder Trilogy is pretty great, even for me who first played them in 2022. It is short enough not to make any game drawn out (my playthrough took about 10 hours per game) while also having a lot of content including a whole short story in the manual of the third game. Fans of newer games such as Legend of Grimrock should find it easy to adjust to this trilogy.
The Trilogy can be played entirely with the keyboard or mouse, or a mix of both.

These games play through DOSBOX and as they are from the first half of the 90s a few QoL features are missing. However, the games do have an automap if you enable it though you might need to change the resolution and play in windowed.

Story
The Eye of the Beholder is not a plot-centred game, you are simply commissioned to find the dangers beneath Waterdeep and if possible, destroy them. That is pretty much the information the intro cutscene and the manual gives you, however, you will learn a lot more during your travels sometimes through talking but mostly through reading notes.
The sequels are a different beast though, the Legend of Darkmoon contains a journal and an investigation report into the Temple Darkmoon with some extra content in the cluebook. Whereas the Assault on Myth Drannor contains an entire short story which introduces a few topics that come up during the course of the game, as well as one of the recruitable NPCs.

Presentation
Visually the screenshots give a fair representation of what the game looks like, I rather like the feel of most enemies, but I was most impressed by the pre-location wallpapers such as the one that appears when you enter Myth Drannor. However, my favourite was the one before the final dungeon, it has an eerie feel reinforced by the short introductory text and the music.
Speaking of… there is not a lot of music in these games, adventuring is very quiet as the songs are mostly saved for important moments such as the aforementioned dungeon entrances or cutscenes. This is a shame since the tracks are generally good dungeon synth, which is pretty fitting, but considering its age its understandable. As for the audio it is usually fine, but nothing outstanding as it sounds a lot like computer noises which is also fair, I guess.

What is less understandable are a few enemies in particular, they are loud, they are annoying, they are never quiet and why did they only make this audio mistake in the Assault on Myth Drannor? I only had such issues with perhaps two enemies in the entire trilogy, but how, why?

Gameplay
The Trilogy is based on AD&D 2nd Edition, so if you have played D&D that is a benefit, but if not the manuals and cluebooks contains information on everything you will need to create a well-balanced party as well as spell descriptions and more.

At most you will be able to create a party of four, but the party limit is six. Naturally, you are given a wide selection of NPCs to recruit in every game, should one not synchronise with the party you always have the option of dropping anyone at any time. However, only four adventurers can carry over to the next game, but you are not forced to keep the ones you have created. I did not keep all four, instead I kept an NPC thief going into the Assault on Myth Drannor since I felt the pain of lacking one in the previous game.
I do wish I was given the option to memorize spells before starting though it is only an issue once per game, but it does become an annoyance if you forgot to do it.

Exploration
An important feature of Dungeon Delvers is dungeon delving, luckily Eye of the Beholder has stuff to explore and puzzles to solve. It is handled a bit differently in terms of layout in every game but in broad strokes it stays the same, in other words a lot of key hunting. That is no issue for me but finding some of the secret buttons can be tricky.
As for the puzzles they can be pretty interesting sometimes requiring items, spells, actions or things I could be forgetting. The Legend of Darkmoon in particular have several that I found entertaining, earlier on I had discarded something I considered useless, but I realised it was the solution. So, I backtracked to where I had dropped the item… only to discover that in the alcove I had not yet explored (next to the puzzle) there were spares. Though for most puzzles you will be fine with the hints you are given especially if you keep everything that looks important. If not the cluebook is included with solutions.

the Legend of Darkmoon also has my favourite dungeon layout, you explore different sections of the temple each having different ways to enter them, but once you have gotten what you need you return to the entrance, and in the midst of all the cultists it starts to feel familiar and safe. The Assault on Myth Drannor does not do this, though I quite like the way it does the dungeons too with them being key buildings in the ruined city and it does succeed in making you feel unwelcome in its streets. With that said the final dungeon of the trilogy is extremely cool and well done, and it is my favourite final stretch in the trilogy.

I will now complain about The Eye of the Beholder 1, it is mostly a pure descent with a few dead ends thrown in, in principle I do not dislike this, but the portals make it worse. There are way too many of them, sixteen of them in fact, and there are seven keys that you need to use them. The game would have been better if portals were used sparingly and not on every floor after the fourth one, it would also have been better if they all connected but it was the key that decided where you travelled, but yeah that is my opinion.

Combat
Eye of the Beholder’s combat is generally the same as what it is adapting, and many other features in it are common in Dungeon Delvers. If you have only played newer games, then you may find that Legend of Grimrock is very much like Eye of the Beholder. Your party move as one and you can freely switch their positions and equipment between them. Meaning you can easily bench a low HP fighter or throw something only to quickly replace it so that you are ready when you engage the enemy in melee.
Unlike AD&D though, this is real-time, which means you will need to be more aware of your surroundings so as not to be surprised by patrols or respawning enemies, the latter case only applies to certain areas. Sometimes your enemies will be reinforced, other times they will activate traps while advancing on you and if you are in a bad spot, you will get surrounded. Would you believe that being flanked is bad thing? However, you can often use reinforcing enemies to your advantage as it can make powerful spells worthwhile instead of a waste of resources.

With that said, what do you do to avoid being stuck in a corner? Well, you step aside, with that simple tricks you can beat the game. This strategy means that the All-Seeing Eye becomes even stronger, as it means you never run the risk of backing into a corner. It becomes a bit too dominant, but luckily its not a strategy that will work in every encounter as some traps can be downright devious.
Some of my favourite sections of these games were early encounters of spiders when you had no spell to cure poison and no potions. The game forces you to advance because the alternative is death. At the time I thought it was frustrating, but that terror is still vivid in my mind. Furthermore, the claustrophobia you feel when you are trapped is also a great feeling, it makes everything so tense, and you know that if you make a mistake, it can cost you dearly. I am less of a fan of the instant death spells, though, and paralysis effects but bad RNG is ultimately a part of Dungeons and Dragons. Could you guess how to avoid such things? By strafing of course.

SHORT REVIEW
Eye of the Beholder I, II & III are a set of 3D first-person perspective Dungeon Crawlers from the early 90's based on the 2nd Edition AD&D rules set. Although I have recommended these games that recommendation does come with a fairly heavy set of caveats which I will detail later.

PLOT: The three games each have their own plots which follow on from one another but as with most dungeon crawlers the plot doesn't feature that heavily in the games themselves.

COMBAT: The combat system is pretty standard for a dungeon crawler but those not familiar with the 2nd Edition AD&D rules set may find some of the underlying mechanics a little “obtuse” to say the least. Also, as older games they don't contain some of the quality of life features of more modern games.

GRAPHICS & SOUND: These games are old (1991-1993). and were written under MS-DOS. They were good for their time but that was 30 years ago so the graphics and sound now seem extremely dated.

REPLAY VALUE: Very little. Then again I'd say that about all dungeon crawlers.

ISSUES AND BUGS: None. The games run “straight out of the box”.

ACHIEVEMENTS AND TRADING CARDS: No integration with the Steam platform.

VALUE FOR MONEY: Average. The games are reasonably priced for what they are but I'd still put it in the “wait for a discount in a sale” category rather than the “buy it now at full price” category. After all, its not like you're missing out on a new release or anything!

LONG REVIEW
Although there is no integration with the main steam platform the Gold Box games come nicely packaged up in their own little launcher which includes all the games (well the ones you own anyway) along with all seeing eye mapping function, controls, instruction manuals and clue books all easily accessible and in one place. No rummaging around in the bowels of steam trying to find out if the instruction manual is lurking somewhere, its all at your fingertips.

I said at the start that although I have recommended these games that recommendation does come with a fairly heavy set of caveats. Basically I would say you should only play these games if you fall into one (or more) of the following categories:

1: Nostalgia value: You played these games in your youth and want a trip down memory lane.
2: Played but never finished: You played these games in your youth and never finished them.
3: Historical interest: You enjoy seeing how dungeon crawlers have developed over the years. (These basically sit straight after Dungeon Master chronologically).
4: Glutton for punishment: Your really like playing old computer games that, in all honesty, are past their “best before” date.

If you don't fall into one of these categories its probably best to give these a miss and play something a bit more modern, these games are really past their “best before” date to be honest.

Also, as older games they don't contain some of the quality of life features of more modern games (e.g. you will spend a LOT of time picking up ammunition as there is no auto pick up function).

Intro jako řemen
Většinou se snažím psát recenze objektivně, snažím se vypíchnout to nejlepší a pokud uznám, že si to hra nezaslouží, tak jí prostě nepochválím, (na rozdíl od většiny novodobých pseudorecenzentů, kteří mnohdy hru ani nehrají nebo nedohrají do konce). Dnes ale udělám výjimku a bude jen chválit, chválit a chválit. A to proto, že každý z nás má svoji nejoblíbenější hru, film a knihu (i když nutně nemusí být nejlepší v oboru). A vy zrovna čtete recenzi o mé nejoblíbenější počítačové hře a to jest… legendární Eye of the Beholder 2: Legend of Darkmoon (dále jen EOB2).

Historie jako řemen
Stará „písíčka“, neexistující internet, počítačové hry, které se šmelily po disketách, Depešáci hrající ve walkmanech, Doomtroperové kartičky a životní objev zvaný Červený trpaslík. Pamatujete si tuto dobu? Jestli ano pak to znamená dvě věci. První – zažili jste devadesátky. Druhá – jste staří!
No, a právě v této době čirou náhodou přijel bratranec a na třiapůlce disketě přinesl hru, kterou jsem hrál cirka dvacetkrát, a ještě mě nějaké to repete čeká. Nutno říci, že jedno z velkých plusek EOB2ky, je právě nostalgie divokých devadesátek, undergroundové prostředí počítačových her a dlouhé hodiny, které jsme se starším bratrancem trávili u této hry. „EOB2 spolu s Golden axem“ byly navíc prvním uvítáním do fantasy světů, které jsem si okamžitě zamiloval. Dost bylo ale historie a vrhněme se na samotnou hru.

Hra jako řemen
EOB2ka vyšla v roce 1991 původně na Amize a má ji na starosti kdysi legendární firma Westwood studios, která má na svědomí další herní pecky jako Legend of Kirandia, Duna série nebo další fantasy sérii Lands o Lore. Serie. EOB obsahuje tři díly a nutno podotknout, že právě druhý díl je nejlepší. Jednička je ještě nedopečená a trojka odfláknutá. Při tvorbě dvojky navíc pomáhalo studio SSI, které se zasloužilo o (v té době) dech beroucí počítačovou grafiku a neopakovatelnou atmosféru. Hlavní designér Brett W. Sperry tehdy odvedl kus práce a má u mě stále schovaného herního Bludišťáka prvního stupně.

Dnes už klasika. Advenced dungeons and dragons setting. Parta čtyř dobrodruhů, kupecké městečko Waterdeep a hlavní mág Khelben „Blackstaff“ Arunsun, který si vás pozve do pracovny a tam stručně vysvětlí, co je potřeba udělat. Nedaleko městečka stojí prastarý klášter „Darkmoon“, kterému šéfuje záporňáckej Dran Draggore a je potřeba velmi rychle tento klášter pročistit, vykuchat a nakopat Drana špičatou botou na Nedělu, do sedinky. Ťoť vše, a i když v klášteře najdete občasné fragmenty příběhu a pár postav, které se k vám přidají o moc víc děje zde nehledejte. V roce 1991 byl EOB2 brán jako právoplatné RPG, ale my bychom ho dnes zařadili do kategorie „krokových dungeonovek“, protože i když je klášter celkem rozmanitý (kobky, věže a bludiště aj.) Celou dobu se z něj nehnete a vylezete až po závěrečném fajtu.

Hra je zobrazena z vlastního pohledu. Můžete mít v partě až šest dobrodruhů, kteří mají své klasické povolání (paladin, mág zloděj aj.). Levlují sbíráním zkušeností v boji, sbírají všemožné předměty a vybavení, řeší množství hádanek a bojují proti potvorám. Nutno podotknout, že boje jsou ještě relativně jednoduché, ale některé hádanky vás opravdu potrápí, a dokonce se může stát, že když hru nešikovně uložíte, dostanete se do fáze hry, ze které není úniku. Udělali jste chybu? Nepřečetli si nápovědu? Tak trpte! Ano i takto drsná hra umí být. Dobrou zprávou ale je, že v Steam verzi EOB2 je zabudován automatický mapper (kdysi addon), který ušetří padesát procent práce… to mi věřte.

Prostředí je poměrně rozmanité, atmosféra pěkná a grafické zpracování můžeme, s přehledem, označit jako „výkladní skříň starých Dosáckých her“, prostě krása. Ve hře jsou navíc občasná setkání, které mají vlastní krásné ilustrace. To napomáhá k dotváření atmosféry. Setkáte se tak tváří v tvář s Beholdry, nemrtváky nebo jinými prudnými bytostmi, na které ale bude radost pohledět. Hudba je sice minimální (cca dvě skladby) ale zvuky a ambientní zvuky dotváří taktéž solidně atmosféru (berte s rezervou s ohledem na tehdejší technické možnosti). Hratelnost úžasná, ale na dnešní dobu poměrně tuhá, i když jak jsem zmínil výše, automatický mapper vám opravdu velmi pomůže. Bez něj jsem se leta trápil i já a občas měl chuť sníst vlastní boty! Jo, a není projevem zbabělosti, když občas šáhnete po návodu, obzvláště pokud chcete najít všechny společníky a nejlepší výbavu.

Závěr jako řemen
Je jasné, že na dnešní dobu, a především pro dnešní hráče je EOB2 za zenitem, ale pokud jste opravdoví herní fajnšmekři a myslíte to s hraním vážně, alespoň na poli dungenů a RPG, je pro vás EOB2 povinnost. Myslím, že tento výlet do devadesátek a zavzpomínání na tehdejší hezké, ale tuhé hry, bude stát zato. Nabrousit meče, nachystat lektvary a zapnout DOSBOX! Pravé hrdinství čeká 10/10.

PS: Forgotten Realms Archives je vlastně soubor starších dosových her ze světa Forgotten Realms, do kterého si můžete dokoupit (formou DLC) další fantasy série. Dost uvažuji, že to skoupím všechno, ale jestli bude čas to vše znovu dohrát… ví Bozi.

PPS: Holky tehdá počítačové hry nehrály!

Just as I remembered from my childhood! This is probably one of the most quintessential, if not THE most quintessential, D&D game series ever made. As an avid gamer, and player of D&D with pencil and paper since the 80's, I can attest that this series is absolutely a must have for any fantasy or D&D fan. With that said, the issues are apparent. It is quite dated as games go, and sadly the third installment is lackluster, due to being made by a different company than the first two games. The other drawback to this pack is that as you play through the games, you will be unable to port your games from one to the next, as you could with the original games.

For the most part though, this is a wonderful walk down memory lane, or a fun and challenging dungeon crawler for any new player. If you enjoy older games, and a fantasy first person dungeon crawler in the spirit and vein of the Wolfenstein series or Doom, this game is probably something you will enjoy. If you have never played this game I would suggest trying without the many walkthroughs available online for these games, but some of the puzzles can be challenging, so I wouldn't disparage anyone for looking. Overall this series was probably one of my favorite games of all time, I played them extensively as a kid and now have revisited them. They are just as good as they were in the 90's.

Eye of the Beholder. Dabei kann einem nur der gleichnamige Song von Metallica einfallen (Aus dem Album …And Justice for All) oder eben aber diese Rollenspieltrilogie von den guten alten Westwood Entwicklern (Command & Conquer). Ich hatte zu der Zeit mit Fantasy Rollenspielen und AD&D nicht viel am Hut am Computer. Da gabs aufm C64 nur das gute alte Ultima. Die anderen Goldboxteile hab ich leider nie gespielt bis auf Menzoberranzan später.
Grade zocktechnisch bin ich in den 90'ern groß geworden und es gab damals zwei Spiele die mich entscheidend prägten und dafür sorgen sollten mich von meinem guten alten Commodore 64 zu verabschieden. Der eine Titel war Wing Commander auf dem PC und der andere Eye of the Beholder. Habe dieses wundervolle Kleinod der Computerspiel Geschichte damals erstmals bei einem Schulfreund auf einem Amiga 2000 bewundern dürfen und mir ist dabei die Kinnlade runtergeklappt.
Die Grafik war der Hammer. Cool aussehende animierte Monster. Guter Sound.
Aus heutiger Sicht mag sich das lächerlich anhören aber EotB war für mich persönlich der Startschuss zu allem anderen was dann später im Bereich AD&D und Fantasyrollenspielen noch kommen sollte. Mitte der 90'er nannte ich dann endlich einen ordentlichen PC mein Eigen und konnte es dann nachholen.
Eye of the Beholder I - III aber auch Dungeon Master I und II. Und Hand aufs Herz EotB war im Endeffekt Dungeon Master in gut aussehend mit AD&D Lizenz was in den 80'ern ja enorm angesagt war. Hab es zu einer Zeit gespielt als Rollenspiele und Fantasygames nicht mehr ganz so in waren. Aber ein Jahr später kam ja Baldurs Gate.

Auf die genauen Einzelheiten und Details von EotB I - III möchte ich nicht weiter eingehen das hat hier schon jemand sehr herrausragend getan. Zu meiner Schande muss ich gestehen keinen einzigen der 3 Teile durchgezockt zu haben.
Eye of the Beholder war bockschwer und es soll mir jetzt keiner ankommen mit früher war das Gras doch viel grüner. Harte Kämpfe, knackige Rätsel. Hier musste man sich die Karte des Dungeons verdammt noch mal selber auf Karopapier zeichnen wenn man sich nicht ständig verfranzen wollte. Im Vergleich zu modernen Spielen hat das schon etwas von SM.
Aber ich habe es geliebt. Und liebe es auch heute noch. Und werde die 3 Teile auch noch mal angehen.
Kann ich auch den ganzen 0815 Zockern und den seichten Smartphonespielern von heute nur empfehlen diese Erfahrung zu machen. Man bekommt hier nichts vorgekaut. Hier gibts kein Ingameshop und nix extra zu kaufen. Es gibt hier keine Highscores und keine Erfolge zur erspielen. Nur den eigenen Erfolg und die persönliche Genugtuung. Und wer z.B Dungeon Master durchgespielt hat (mit Zeugen bitte) darf sich das ohne weiteres auf seinem Grabstein eingravieren lassen. Alles Spiele eben aus einer anderen Zeit. Aus einer sehr schönen Zeit was Computerspiele angeht.

Ich möchte mich noch dafür bei denjenigen bedanken die es ermöglicht haben diese Klassiker auf Steam zu bringen.
Mir ist das Herz aufgegangen.

Und wenn wir schon dabei sind:
Dungeon Master 1 und 2 könnt hier auch gerne mal reinkloppen. Ebenso Panzer General (1994) von SSI. Eines der besten
Rundenstrategiespiele überhaupt. Ist 2021 von Index gegangen.

Und was AD&D Rollenspiele angeht:
The Temple of Elemtal Evil von Troika Games (2003).
