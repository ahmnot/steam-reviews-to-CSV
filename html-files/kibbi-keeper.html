I finally got around to playing this game and figured I'd give my thoughts on it while it's fresh in my head:

For starters, I have to mention the obvious that the Kibbis are adorable. In general the game gives off super relaxing cutesy vibes that make it easy to just sit down and play through. The game is just visually amazing and I was nearly fully immersed in the experience because of it. The music as well is very fitting and I enjoyed the dynamic aspect of it based on proximity to your Kibbis, though it can drone on a little bit since it's constant.

Next, I really enjoyed some of the open and interconnected design of the world. Gates between areas made sense and the way that some shortcuts open up and loop back into areas you've already been to is fantastic. The puzzles themselves are simple to understand and convey really well, and I didn't solve any of them in a way that I felt was breaking them.

That said, my entire experience wasn't completely positive. I had quite a few points of frustration with how the Kibbis would behave at times. Sometimes the Kibbis would refuse to follow me or have trouble navigating where I wanted them to go. The Kibbis themselves are also very slow compared to the player, so you can spend a lot of time waiting around for them to do what you want them to do.

My biggest point of contention was with the hunger mechanic. Early on it isn't as much of a deal, there are plenty of fruit around and only one or two Kibbi's to take care of. However once you get to managing like 4 or more Kibbis having enough fruit can be a nightmare, and some of fruit in the trees are very hard to see. I feel like a parent who has had or adopted too many kids and has to rush to get food to feed them all. Oh and for the LOVE of GOD don't let them go fully hungry because they will let out the most blood-curdling scream I've ever heard!

Other than that, there were a few bugs here and there, mostly with bridges not seeming to stay up after pressing levers and leaving the area, and I maybe had one instance where I got to a place I don't think I should've been, but overall nothing major.

Overall, for me, I really enjoyed my time with this game for the first hour, but it started to make me feel a bit more exhausted and frustrated after that point. However, I do also understand that I am not entirely the target audience for this game. All that said, the student devs made an amazing experience here, a pretty dense one too, and they should be proud of what they accomplished. I give this game the solid Legendanny seal of approval!
