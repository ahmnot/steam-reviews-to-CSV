Pros:
Excellent Atmosphere
Feeling of Isolation
Great Premise

♥♥♥♥:
Tedious Collecting
Lack of Direction for Key Item Locations
Extremely Limited Inventory

Dead Man's Diary starts strong, you're abandoned in the middle of nowhere after you were chosen by the bunker you were staying in to be thrown out due to lack of supplies. With no where to go you head anywhere where food, water, and shelter is located. Since this all takes place after a nuclear holocaust there's no one around creating a sense of isolation as you jump at every small sound and wonder if something is stalking you.

The developers did a great job creating that sense of seclusion but this excitement is quickly overshadowed by tedious collecting. You have to collect supplies to maintain your hunger, thirst, radiation levels, and regulate heat but since most food and water is radiated you'll have to scan everything before it can be used for use or in crafting. The problems surface when you're given little direction of where these key items are located and the constant need to rebuild your camp.

The opening level provides arrows to give you direction but once gone you start to notice how poorly things are telegraphed. For example you're told to find the keys to a locked door but given no idea where they are or where to begin your search. This goes for blankets, wood, matches, and so many things that you'll wander around in circles just hoping this this specific wood pile is what you need all while your survival gauges continue to drain.

You can make the argument that this is a survival game so locating items in random locations is part of the experience. But when you spend 1 hour looking for blankets for another camp because your character decided to abandon perfectly good supplies at a previous camp you start to become annoyed when he does it over and over again. Having to find the same things, having to comb over every small area, and realizing you did locate the item but the grab item never appeared. At that moment there's no feeling of relief for finding that 1 necessary item, it's anger.

It's a shame because the visual design and atmosphere is well done, especially the lighting. But once you spend an hour looking for 1 key to open a door only to find out you need to locate another item that's not in the area you're in, that's when you throw your hands in the air and say "I'm Done".

In-Depth
Premise, Setting & Writing
More than a decade after nuclear warfare scorched the planet, you get the short end of the stick at your bunker's lottery, to exile a number of survivors because supplies can't possibly last for everyone anymore. Abandoned in the wild with only minimal equipment, you'll undertake a survivor's arduous way of life, and embark on a journey though toxic sewers, abandoned hospitals, deserted cities and more locales.

Despite the relative freshness of atomic doomsday, somehow the planet readily sprung back to life: verdant forests teeming with wildlife sounds, fields of grass and flowers blooming won't be uncommon sights... they should be. The apocalyptic portrayal of the world is lackluster, as not only it's nowhere near as desolate as it should, but also no corpses can ever be seen, save for some in plastic bags at specific locations. Animals aren't mutated while structures, buildings and so-forth appear blemished by time but mostly undamaged. Clearly the Developers have no idea, whatsoever, how a nuclear apocalypse would actually affect the world and its infrastructure.

For a game betting it all on atmosphere, the visuals may even do the part, but the writing doesn't. The various notes found in the world prove uninteresting most of the time, fail to recant the stories of those who came before in any involving manner. The protagonist himself is a cringe bag of one-liners that tries to be funny but, most of the time, just sounds like a frustrated basement dweller scorned at every minimal impasse.

General Gameplay
The main concern at all times will, of course, be survival. Several gauges like hunger, thirst, radiation and more will constantly deplete, ensuring death when zero is reached. To keep them at bay, a constant scavenging work needs to be done, opening the numerous containers scattered in each level, or simply picking up whatever supply lies scattered around, such as drinks, food, iodine pills. Nourishment may be contaminated, and needs to be scanned with a Geiger counter each time - a dull task, when you have to do so every single time for each individual piece. Many containers, doors, lockers will be secured, needing lockpicks to open them, in a well-known minigame.

The other objective will be getting to the next area, which usually entails finding the right set of keys, passwords, resources or whatever else, in order to make your way though. Building a campsite is often a requirement to do so, with rare resources like bedrolls, camping stoves, hay bales and more being scattered throughout each map, often without a logical sense or any hint about their location. Prepare to explore every inch, as guidance after the first few levels becomes non-existent.

While the survival needs won't be much of a threat for the accurate looter, searching for the key items will prove painstakingly boring, as the Devs often decide to place them in devious, extremely hidden locations that don't make any sense at all, or bar them behind puzzles with obscure solutions. Passing over and over already-seen locales to find the proverbial "needle in a haystack" will be your daily routine. Despite the presence of some variations like timed sequences, chases and stealth parts, these are extremely limited in duration compared to the total gameplay which is, for 95% of the time, the same slog copied over and over.

With very limited inventory capacity and needs constantly draining, you'll have to scavenge all the time to not expire.

Combat System
Only much later will you get a hold of firearms, to fend off animals and other more sinister threats. Headshots generally dispatch all enemies instantly, however guns prove difficult to aim due to sensitivity issues and bullet trajectory being slightly offset. Also, bullets disappear after a certain range, so forget long-range engagements. Firefights prove basic, against melee-only enemies: backpedaling while shooting often does the job, and ammo is plentiful where there's a need for it. An addition that feels welcome after hours of painful redundancy, but mediocre onto itself.

Puzzles
Mainly involve deducting safe combinations from notes or the environment, repairing circuit boards with resistors, or cracking computers like a true hacker. It's quite hilarious that our protagonist seems to know enough to be able to hack military computers or repair complex circuits, however the game doesn't actually explain how these puzzles work at all, or at best, sparingly. Prepare to be frustrated at incomprehensible circuits or password-forcing tools that don't have any hint on how to approach them, while your survival needs (and your patience...) drain constantly.

The safe combinations aren't any better, oftentimes requiring you to find insanely-placed hints or operate matrices / binary conversion, which is far from common knowledge - you will, definitely need the aid of Internet to gather such notions at some point (I used a binary to decimal converter, for instance). Puzzles may vary in shape or size but are always the same four types all the time, for 24 hours, on top of everything else already mentioned. The epitome of dullness, truly.

Especially if you decide to play this game. He definitely can't.

Following the story was interesting enough to keep me playing, even if the survival mechanics and wayfinding were annoying right from the start.

The character needs water and food literally every few minutes.
This feels excessive, but if you somehow manage to keep this needy man alive, you'll also have to deal with his declining health.
On top of that, there is radiation in the air. As a result, you will need to take iodine pills regularly and you will also need to scan every type of food and water that you can find, while half of it is radiated and unusable.

Sounds hardcore, right? It is.

Then there are the puzzles. Some are explained, some give you a scheme with no further or helpful explanation. Therefore, you basically try to force your way through it until you solve it, or rely on a guide from someone who also struggled to solve this puzzle.
If that's not entertaining enough, while you're in the puzzle menu, your health / food / water needs keep decreasing. So, sometimes you'll finish a puzzle, but because you took too long, you'll die.

Of course for masochistic people this must be wonderful, but for a casual like me that's hell and certainly not adding fun to the gameplay experience.

All the developers would have to do, is add some proper difficulty settings, where we can change the characters needs from "super needy baby" to "regular human being".

If the developers add a difficulty setting, I would totally recommend buying it, but not in its current state.

Dead Man´s Diary ist ein Survival-Game von TML-Studios, was auf einer völlig neuen Spielmechanik basiert, wie ich sie bisher noch nie erlebt habe! Als Ausgestoßener startet man in einer post-apokalypische Welt, die nach einen nuklearen Krieg, auf den man voll und ganz auf sich allein gestellt ist und die Hauptaufgabe ist klar: Überleben!

Mit einer beeindruckenden gruseligen Atmosphäre und grafischen Augenschmaus nimmt das Spiel mich auf eine spannenden Reise voller Gefahren, Geschichten und den Drang zu Überleben mit!

Positiv ist mir das einfache und übersichtliche Crafting aufgefallen, wo man durch das sammeln der Ressourcen, das ein oder andere Lager aufbauen muss, um wichtige Mittel wie Medizin, Essen und andere wichtige Dinge herzustellen, die für das Überleben wichtig ist. Auch die Ausrüstungsgegenstände können im weiteren Spielverlauf durch simples Handling auf einer Werkbank upgraded werden.

Auch die packende Begegnung von Gegnern und Rätselaufgaben bietet das Spiel eine gelungenen Abwechslung, die sich von Anfang des Spiels bis Ende schrittweise steigert.

Insgesamt kann ich sagen, ist Dead Man´s Diary ein sehr gelungenes Spiel, mit allen was zum Thema "Survival" gehört und kann dies mit voller Zufriedenheit weiterempfehlen!

Die für mich wichtige Anmerkungen zum Spiel:

- spannende Storyline
- Übersichtliche Menüführung
- gut erklärendes Tutorial
- Sehr gute Grafik
- hervorragende Atmosphäre und Sounds

总体上来看

本作是一款偏向解密和物品收集的垃圾佬游戏，玩家需要四处开箱子，捡垃圾，找便签，搞物资，按照任务提示完成建筑物建造和物品收集，战胜一些敌人以便有更大的活动范围。对喜欢当垃圾佬的收集癖玩家来说应该很有趣，对普通玩家可能略显单调和无聊。只推荐收集和解密游戏爱好者入手，其他玩家谨慎入手。

下面是具体的评测内容

垃圾佬的快乐时光

对于那些喜欢收集物资的玩家来说，游戏内到处都是值得收集利用的物资这一点应该很能让他们有获得感和满足感。使用道具打开箱子获得计数器之后，各种物资就不再只是观赏品，可以被带走了。

游戏内虽然到处都是木棍和钢管，但是只有特定的物品才能搭造帐篷，这种游戏体验挺独特的，应该能满足少部分喜欢收集和解密玩家的需求，而对我这种普通玩家来说……就摁拖时间是吧？

丰富但是体验不佳的游戏内容

在找不到物资之后，我决定跑去云通关了——b站up们的反馈和我出齐的一致，都是打一下然后就放弃了，目前视频最新进度是第二关。

他们打的速度比我快很多，找物资也很得力，表现的也很好，比我强到不知道哪里去了，但是从他们的游戏过程和评论区反馈来看……

好像他们也不觉得这款游戏真的很好玩，有什么值得分享出来给大家通关提供一定的帮助。

我又看了看评论区，发现不是我一个人对这款游戏有体验不佳的感受，大家对这款游戏的意见应该都比较统一——内容挺丰富的，看起来很有趣，玩起来嘛……玩起来的体验就是……粗鄙之语，退钱！
