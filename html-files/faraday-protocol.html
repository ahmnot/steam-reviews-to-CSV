Faraday Protocol is a first-person puzzle game where you play as an intergalactic archaeologist exploring an abandoned planet, encountering an AI who leads him into ziggurats which contains puzzles. The aesthetics are pretty unique, black-gold ancient Egyptian style rooms and statues. The puzzle mechanic is you have a gun that can carry red/blue energy, and with it you must progress these trials.

Gameplay-wise, its pretty similar to room-based puzzle titles such as Turing Test, Spectrum Retreat, and most famously Portal. However, I did not enjoy this title as much as those. The puzzles can be hard, annoying, and confusing to figure out. The rooms, while it does look pretty at first, start to get monotonous and boring. I only start enjoying this game near the end, where the puzzles get simpler and you get more story content.

Sadly, I cannot recommend Faraday Protocol based on my tastes. However, if you enjoy puzzle games, especially the aforementioned titles more than I do, I think its worth a try. Just don't expect a story as good as Portal though. I've seen this game go through several updates from the guide videos I used, would be nice if more "story rooms" were added and the puzzles less hard.
