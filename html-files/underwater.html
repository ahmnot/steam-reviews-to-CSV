Here's The Thing:
You are nobody... or rather you can't remember who you are? Waking up to the aftermath of scientists, doctors and guards being obliterated- or maybe they’re prisoners just playing dress-up!? Anyway, it's up to you to find the way out with some notes left by some rando who's totally not trying to fix his mistakes~


[+] PROS;

:) VISUALS
Granted, there isn’t much to look at because of how dark everything is… But that don’t mean it’s a bad-looking game. On the contrary, the penitentiary is actually well-designed and believable! Though I still don’t know the main agenda of this institution or who I played as- I was interested in the monsters, ghosts whose existence tell me it was something not good I bet!

I may be wrong, but I think their models could be original creations! I have never seen them before and they have quite a lot of detail to them. Even the corpses are not too shabby looking despite being you know lifeless and contorted. The lighting and shadows is especially done justice and I like the way my shadow gets casted, even spooking me at times~


:) HORROR
While the gameplay’s a little monotonous and uninspired, the horror will at least keep you awake and on the edge of your seat. Going in, you won’t know what to expect (apart from what the back of your mind is concocting up). I actually freaked myself out more times than the actual scares UDR had in store for me. The dev has a way of preying on your fears, and it’s pretty darn subtle. The way notes are pinned to the wall and set up the situation. For instance, it tells you an enemy is ahead and it’s not too smart but will detect you eventually.

Or someone is infected and locked in a room for being aggressive. Then, the key item is in there with him- how would you react and where do you go? Most of the horrific bits are laid out in plain sight and will catch you only if you let yourself be vulnerable to them! It was truly unconventional and I wished there had been more of it. Even the way you’re supposed to handle each enemy was unspoken and I liked working out how they ticked…
No jumpscares but there can be surprises if you don't pay attention to your surroundings or read the notes. Tub of lard was hidden... ^

:( LACKS DEPTH...
It’s a short-lived, formulaic and brief playthrough, like 30 minutes give or take 10! Seeing as you’ll finish it once- won’t ever need to play it again because it’s a key-finding simulator with puzzle codes that do not randomize, fixed enemy placements and with no difficulty modes.

Oh and you know those burning questions in the trailer? The ones that were posed to you just begging to be answered?? You can forget all about those! I can sum it up in two words- nothing happens!. Which is an utter disgrace, because it had that little likeness to Outlast in scenarios, setting and maybe the hostiles… but could not be leagues apart!! Possibly some resemblance to Cabin in The Woods too, but I guess we’ll never know now, would we~


:( BLOATED CONTROLS
The main character’s movements and actions are as if his oxygen is running out or maybe he also has a lumpy torso like the mascot of the game. Acts such as taking out the flashlight get him winded, presumably, as he won’t take it out when he’s near and staring at the wall.

Even when working fine, there’s a 3 second buffer between being able to switch it back on again after switching off! Which translates to bashing your head in the wall since you’ll be running in pitch black a lot while being chased~


:( CLUNKY DESIGN
Usually saving gives relief but not here, since there’s no telling what exactly it saves or when. To help clear things, there are essentially two forms; one major checkpoint / scripted event save and this is where you’ll be spawned when you croak.

The other saves progression of items and is done in the background, although you will see an indication of it. Flashback to that part where a crazed druggy charged at me as soon as I loaded in even though I had already locked him in a separate room and had many saves after doing so.

Which brings me to my next point of how the enemies have that “catch you off guard” attribute when you first meet but outrunning them becomes all too simple once you realize they’re confined to their zones. They don’t react to neither sound nor vision unless you’re parading yourselves directly in front of them. AND they have like real slow movement speed and arm swings… It’s super hard to be scared by them~

~ Doesn't save my reconfigured keyboard mapping.

Некоторые ужастики не выполняют своей основной цели — пугать людей. Разработчики думают, что пара скримеров, пугающие звуки низкого качества, приведения, людоеды, монстры, зомби, охотящиеся на тебя со смешной анимацией могут привести человека в ужас, из-за чего появляется пот на лбу, а сердце начинает биться чаще, как будто сейчас выскочит из груди.
