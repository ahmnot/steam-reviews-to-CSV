One important note is that even though this is an amateur project, it does seem to be sincerely and genuinely made. I couldn't find any flipped assets, plagiarism or any other kind of insincere actions from the developer, but unfortunately genuine intentions alone are not enough to produce a brilliant PC gaming experience.

Probably the real killer for the game is just the low production values and lack of polish here. The game assets are really poor, controls clunky, and the puzzles require a lot of awkward placement as you fight more against the bad control scheme and poorly implemented physics than the puzzles themselves.

From a technical perspective, the game doesn't meet basic minimum requirements that most PC gamers expect as standard.

The game features lazy minimalist/poorly textured low-polygon "retro" assets and visuals, making this look like a barely functional 3D game from the early 1990s. The lack of quality textures is a method that lazy devs often use to disguise their lack of talent/interest in doing the graphics properly and trying to disguise it under the name of "art", or "We made it look bad on purpose", which really isn't something gamers should have to put up with. It's unclear why the developers weren't willing to arrange high quality, high polygon count contemporary assets and high resolution textures for the game. It looks bad as a result of their decisions, and that's just another reason to avoid it.

The poor quality of this game is reflected by how many people spent time with it. At the time of this review, SteamDB shows the all-time peak player number was only 7 players. This is a remarkably low number, and now, the only player activity occurs once or twice a month, presumably someone loading it up to see what it is then quickly uninstalling it. Considering there's over 120 million gamers on Steam and well over 50,000 games for gamers to choose from (over 9,000 completely free titles), the overwhelming lack of interest in this low quality game is to be expected.

The God Unit has the utterly unrealistic price of $10 USD, for which you could easily get any number of older AAA games. Given the defects and quality issues with the game, coupled with the unrealistic price, this is impossible to recommend. For comparison, the $10 asking price for this game could get you games like "Europa Universalis IV", "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt" or "Rust". This puzzle game apes games like Portal and Q.U.B.E, but costs twice as much as those games!. Quality, professionally made games like those are frequently on sale cheaper than this. There's also thousands of free, superior quality games on Steam.

全45レベルでノーマルとハードモードがある。
ハードモードでは部屋の構造そのものは余り変化しないが、配置されている仕掛けや手順が異なり、高難度な別解を要求されるので当然ノーマルより難しい。ノーマルだと、スイッチを押したらドアがずっと開いているが、ハードだと数秒でドアが閉まってしまうから駆け込みに間に合うための工夫をする必要があるなど・・

両モード合わせると実質的に90近いレベルが用意されてるので、値段の割にはかなりボリュームがあると思います。実際、ノーマルモードクリアまで17－18時間程度掛かりましたし、ハードクリアにはそれ以上の時間が掛かるかもしれません。

気になった点
良くも悪くも物理オブジェの挙動次第なので、スマートにパズルが解けないこともある。質量を変えるためのマウスホイールをゴリゴリする使用頻度が高めでちょっと面倒。本来通過できないフィールドをあるオブジェを無理やり貫通させてしまうバグがある。本来は謎解きに関係ない、壁の装飾の様な出っ張りにキューブが引っかかった事や、キャラ自体も乗れてしまう。ハードだと詰んでしまう場面があるため手動ロードが必要になる場合も。

I'm about halfway through the game so far. Listing the things I want to note in order from great to less enjoyable.

  1. Puzzles. They're really good. Clever, interesting, often with many steps and components, but can be reasoned about. So far, I haven't been stuck on the puzzle part of any level, the only real troubles I had were about execution.
  2. Usability / convenience features. Levels often have a bunch of buttons, light sensors, etc., but they're marked with pictograms telling you e.g. which forcefield will be disabled by this button. Also, there's a zoom feature, a hotkey to show jump pad trajectories and a few other convenient things (it's worth looking through the options menu).
  3. Story. Story bits are given rather sparsely and somewhat remind me of Talos Principle. I like what I've learned so far.
  4. Humor. I liked most of the jokes, but I don't think everyone will do so.
  5. There are some rough edges. Occasionally you discover that a button in a different room that should be held by a cube somehow got released. A second or two later it's pressed again, but you've already started thinking whether it's possible to backtrack or you'd have to restart the level.
  6. Physics can be frustrating at times. When moving cubes, it's easy to knock over ones you carefully placed before/ Every now and then, you encounter a level requiring a precision throw, and these have been my nightmare (well, I'm generally not great at things requiring precision or timing). There is a hard mode, where some puzzles were made more difficult (or just more frustrating, as the game honestly warns you), and I gave up literally on the first nontrivial level: I solved the puzzle part, but just couldn't execute the last step.

Overall, definitely recommend the game.
