"My world is slowly collapsing... Everyone I loved is leaving me or betraying me. No one understands me...." - such thoughts often visited every teenager. But what to do if these "inner thoughts" begin to parasitize deeper and deeper, turning into your worst nightmares?

    Liked:
  • + Availability: the game is free
  • + Great idea with non-Euclidean spaces
  • + Nice gameplay idea with photos
  • + Excellent optimization
  • + Several types of "monsters"
  • + Interesting tasks
  • + Great drawings
  • + Good graphics

    Did not like:
  • - Lack of video settings
  • - "He/she" this newfangled "I am a non-binary person" has already made its way here. Abomination.
  • - Small font documents
  • - There is no possibility to "zoom in" an object for research or reading a note

As a result:
Quite an interesting game, or rather one of the chapters of the game - which is available at the time of writing the review.
The gameplay is simple: in the role of a non-binary person (WHY DID YOU WRITE THIS?) we have to go into the child's subconscious through his nightmares. Encountering some nightmares along the way - that torment the child in reality. Along the way, we will try to find out the true causes of these fears and help you "accept" them.
The game has an interesting mechanics of "space", which is fixated on itself. Also, the player has the opportunity to "break" the perspective using photos - which needs to be combined with a place on the level - so that a new passage appears.
At the moment there are only a few "monsters" in the game that will bother you during the game session - which is unfortunately quite short and is about 40-60 minutes. However, I hope that in the next chapters of the game - there will be new nightmares.
I recommend this "prologue" to fans of non-standard puzzles

Since the developers did not want to give us the opportunity to change AT LEAST the screen resolution, we will do it ourselves:
If the game works with lags, or there are friezes, I advise you to change the game settings through the configuration file (GameUserSettings.ini), which is located in C:\Users\USERNAME\AppData\Local\ProjectNox\Saved\Config\WindowsNoEditor
In addition to the screen resolution, you can change other graphics settings here.

9/10 A 1st person puzzle game where you walk around rooms with changing gravity and curved spaces.

Good:
- free (prologue/Chapter 1)
- original idea of the puzzles. They write that it's Escher but really it's not. Escher drew pictures of impossible geometry that looked okay at first glance in 2D. But in this game you have rooms that have each of the 6 sides act like a floor with objects (chairs, tables) standing on them, and you can crawl onto a wall like a cockroach using a set of stairs or a ramp. Rooms rotate to match your position so that you'd always walk up straight. It's hard to explain until you see and try it yourself. It's definitely novel and worth a look
- 2D art (pictures on walls, sheets of paper and much more) is very imaginative. It depicts some crazy kid's life
- 3D objects are great as well. There are chairs that walk like spiders. There are paper airplanes suspended in the air. The design of spaces is just fantastic, it reminds an artistic installation. Not sure I've ever seen such interesting spaces in a game before. 3D design of the rooms is the highlight of this game

So-so:
- it's mostly about walking. So it feels lacking in actual gameplay. During walking you often don't really know what you are doing. Though the first level is pretty straightforward and you can't miss the correct way. So it ends up confusing and shocking for a while, though you generally feel that not much depends on you and you go the correct way and only see afterwards it was correct
- the common puzzle is when you pick up a photo and need to find a 2D view of a wall or corridor that matches the photo, except on the photo there is an additional door or a corridor - and those appear after you've matched the photo to the correct location. This minigame is somewhat annoying after a while

Bad:
- the "run" scene took me at least 6 attempts, maybe more. Then there is a "follow" scene which I couldn't get through after some 8-10 attempts. I'm not sure if it's bugged or just too difficult, or what's even required there. Not sure it's the end of chapter 1. The "run" scene is quite annoying because it's difficult enough to figure out the 3D geometry, and it here it forces you to do it quickly. Maybe timed tasks aren't a good fit for this convoluted geometry context

Overall, it seems to be a very interesting and unique idea coming from artists. But so far it mostly relies on the first "wow" effect and then on the 2D/3D art. There isn't much gameplay in this, and the first excitement fades. I'm not sure what the puzzles could be here. It feels like a good demo but misses something to be a finished experience. Hopefully they add something in the full game. Giving 9/10 because it's still an incredible experience which is definitely worth checking out just to appreciate the geometry and innovative idea.
