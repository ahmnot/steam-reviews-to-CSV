Journey for Elysium by Mantis Games is an adventure story in the afterlife. On the way to Elysium we have to master various tasks.

Journey for Elysium begins quite unselfconsciously. Until we are driven to our deaths by a voice and a floating blue glowing sphere .....
Embedded in a world of Greek mythology consisting almost entirely of shades of grey, we now run and row down the river Styx and learn more and more about the real circumstances of our death. We are now accompanied by what is described as a divine orange orb.

Journey For Elysium casts its spell on us immediately. The environments and the level design are very well done and we enjoy looking around, enjoying the surreal world on the banks of the river. The graphics with their muted colours help to clarify the somehow threatening character of the in-between world.
It is especially great that, since we are on a river, we have to row most of the time. Of course, this is not as realistic as in Mirage Kayak, but our boat is easy to steer across the Styx. But there is also action on land, we have to climb a lot and high, we get a bow and arrow and even a harp to solve certain puzzles. Playing the harp sounds angelic and is fun. I haven't played such a heavenly instrument in VR before.

Basically, in Journey for Elysium we often only have to bring an object to a certain place so that we can move on, but the action that is usually involved compensates for the simple tasks. To make matters worse, the objects all glow cheerfully in a bright orange light, making it hard for us to miss them.
This also makes the game feel more like a kind of narrated film in which we ourselves are the protagonist rather than a pure action adventure. This is exactly how Journey for Elysium is meant to be experienced, and so the experience works very well.
The only downer is that the game is entirely in English and only offers English subtitles. However, the story is understandable with good school English and the game can probably be finished without background knowledge, but then it is not so surprising.
If you don't want to rush through the game, it takes just under 3h. Thanks to the practical chapter selection, however, you don't have to play the game in one go.

Journey for Elysium is a great short narrative night-death experience with an interesting story and a not necessarily foreseeable ending. Recommendation.
