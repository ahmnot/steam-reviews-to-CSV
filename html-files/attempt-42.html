Good concept with bad controls.

First person platform puzzler
In Attempt 42 you have to touch a number of blocks to finish a level. Whenever you are standing on a block, you have the ability to rotate the world over one axis. Basically you're always jumping or falling from one block to another, especially in the time mode in which you have to finish within a sharp time. The world also turns when you follow a curved platform, or when you approach a block from its side.

Controls & conclusion
Mouse & keyboard controls are horrific, and are the reason for my thumbs down. The mouse sensitivity is way too low, although the developer included a slider at some point, it's still garbage! The jump button often doesn't work. Maybe this all works well with a controller, I haven't tried, but this should just work with m+k.
And that's a shame because I did like the minimalistic style of the game. The white surroundings could use more variety. Some puzzles are tight for time, especially with the controls being in the state they are.

It's a puzzle platformer, so I guess I should say it does what it says it'll do... It's just that I don't like it very much at all and I don't know if anyone would like this. So how does it work? What do you do?

You start out on a series of blocks with one highlighted in purple/pinkish color. You step on that block and it turns white. While standing on it, you can rotate the blocks around so that you can jump from one set of blocks to the next and find the next block to step on before you jump to the yellow exit square and finish the level.

The paths curve around and you stick to the path so even if it looks like you're going to walk off of a cliff you're fine (so long as the path curves). Otherwise if you walk off the side of the path you will just fall to your death. If you're lucky you might be able to jump from one path to the next or fall from the high path to a lower path but that's about the extent of the puzzles from what I've seen.

The first level made sense with how you rotated everything and it matched up with the next set of bricks so you could continue through the level. The second level didn't work that way at all and it just felt like they threw the "puzzle" together randomly and called everything "Series A, level 1-10" or whatever. Other than the fact that every level pretty much looks like every other level, there's no real "flow" to the way the levels are designed. In fact I don't even think you have to play the levels in order so really it's like a series of single one-off puzzles rather than a cohesive game.

The music is kinda repetitive and manic sounding without feeling like it belongs in the game. The sound effects are just a "blip" when you change the highlighted square to white and when you exit the level.

Controls are just WASD and mouse to move, jump and look, and Q and E to rotate everything. There's only one axis to rotate things on too so you effectively only have 4 different ways to move your stuff around to set you up for the next block to touch. Maybe they add some more block types and movement options later but I really wasn't motivated to find these later levels to see if they did or if it's just more of the same.

It's disorienting, it's not very much fun, and the whole thing feels really bland. I'd give it a 4/10. If you took this game concept and gave it to some talented level designers who could organize things nicely into a good difficulty curve, and if it allows you to add moves and skills to your repertoire then it would be a much better game. Instead it feels like they came up with a concept and then gave up on level design and just moved to publish it so they could start raking in the big bucks.

I got it with a coupon and I'm now thinking that I don't even want to play this long enough to farm the cards out of it. I can always leave it running for a few hours if I have to, but is it worth it? (shrug)
