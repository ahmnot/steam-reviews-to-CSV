"Attempt[42]" ist ein FIrst-Person Puzzle Spiel.

Wir befinden uns auf einer Plattform die mitten in einem Raum schwebt. Ziel ist es stets, ein oder mehrere Würfel zu aktivieren und dann den Endwürfel zu berühren, um das Level zu schaffen. Dafür müssen wir planen, gehen, springen und auch mal die Perspektive wechseln.

Denn wenn wir auf einem aktivierten Quadrat stehen, können wir den ganzen Raum um uns und damit auch die anderen Plattformen im Raum drehen. Ist unsere Plattform so gebaut, können wir über eine Steigung auch Ebenen betreten, die eigentlich um 90 Grad geneigt erscheinen und vorher für uns eine Wand waren. Im Endeffekt gilt, solange wir auf der Plattform bleiben, immer, daß wir die Schwerkraft definieren, unabhängig davon wie die Ebene gerade gedreht ist. Vermeiden müsssen wir natürlich, von der Plattform zu fallen, denn einen Sturz in die Tiefe überleben wir nicht.

Eigentlich ein gutes Konzept, das allerdings zwei nicht unwesentliche Probleme hat. Zum Einen ist das selbst propagierte Prinzip der durch den stehenden Spieler definierten Schwerkraft nicht komplett durchgehalten. Insbesondere an Steigungsstellen, wo die Schwerkraft eigentlich drehen müßte, gibt es Unklarheiten beim Übergang. Springt man dann auch noch, weiß das Programm gar nicht mehr, was Sache ist. Also sollte man in jedem Fall bedacht und bewußt vorgehen, sonst macht einem das Spiel selbst einen Strich durch die Rechnung. Zumindest kann man die Mausempfindlichkeit, die zunächst sehr merkwürdig eingestellt ist, frei umstellen.

Zum Zweiten ist das Spiel erstaunlich unübersichtlich. Daß man dreidimensional denken muß, ist Teil des Spielprinzips, allerdings ist die FOV-Einstellung so ausgestaltet, daß man sich nach jeder Drehung erstmal komplett im Level orientieren muß, denn man erkennt verdammt wenig und hat nie einen richtig guten Überblick. Das liegt auch daran, daß die meisten Level sehr verschachtelt, aber von der Ausdehnung nicht sehr groß sind. Dadurch sieht man immer nur einen kleinen Teil des Levels gleichzeitig.

Grafisch ist das Unity-Spiel genretypisch sehr einfach. Alle Spielflächen sind farblich und durchsichtig gehalten. Die monotone Musik kann nicht wirklich überzeugen.

"Attempt[42]" hat ein interessantes Konzept. Leider führt die undurchdachte Ausführung dazu, daß man schon leidensfähig sein sollte, um mit dem Spiel Spaß zu haben.

Fazit:

The paths curve around and you stick to the path so even if it looks like you're going to walk off of a cliff you're fine (so long as the path curves). Otherwise if you walk off the side of the path you will just fall to your death. If you're lucky you might be able to jump from one path to the next or fall from the high path to a lower path but that's about the extent of the puzzles from what I've seen.

The first level made sense with how you rotated everything and it matched up with the next set of bricks so you could continue through the level. The second level didn't work that way at all and it just felt like they threw the "puzzle" together randomly and called everything "Series A, level 1-10" or whatever. The music is kinda repetitive and manic sounding without feeling like it belongs in the game. The sound effects are just a "blip" when you change the highlighted square to white and when you exit the level.

The customary unnecessary intro

This game seemed interesting at first, did have steam cards, and well, overall seemed kind of promising. Not sure if I would ever buy it of the shelve thou. To be honest, probably not, but strangely I wouldn’t mind playing the demo of the second version, as the game is promising if things are improved, and the concept of the game is better. Ok, lets get into it.

The Game

The games is a mixture between a puzzle solver and se gravity action game, which very much reminds to games such as Valve's Portal logic, without the graphics and the budget to do most things else. The game is set out as an First Person Shooter ("FPS"). It took some time before the mouse was configured in a playable manner. The first impression was that it moved a bit slow, and the goal or objective of the game was not immediately obvious, at least to me, but I have been known to take more time to understand this kind of games. I hate tutorials as much as the next guy, but when you are an indie, maybe you make things a bit more user friendly at the start of the game. No, it is not that I haven’t played FPS before -I am totally acquainted with the WASD controls- it is more about not exactly knowing what is to be done at the start of the game. Every game has its learning curve, but some clues, some more context to what is going in in the surroundings or in the game’s universe itself would be of great help, if it's not too much to ask, that is.

A concept is fine

Some say that giving a game is virtually unimportant and that the story is for dreamers, peeps who are not really into gaming and maybe they are right. I consider myself one of those guys who can just go on with some imagination. I won’t read all the text conversations like a book neither, but I really like the experience a game can deliver, and I play them with some expectancy. So, when there is very little context and intro in a game I am somewhat disappointed. Even when you are creative, even fantastically committed, you will eventually need some support to help your mind wander of deep into a game. This did definitely not happen with me with this game; it was a good concept at the beginning, but it all became a bit repetitive and obvious. The challenges do get more complicated as you advance from level to level, but there is also a sensation that the level design was a bit wary.

Conclusions

For a game of which the basic goal is shifting gravity to move from point A to point B (some variations, of course) it is all right, but I truly think that the game could have been much more if they gave it that little extra. It is recognizable that even thou it is an indie, it is always smooth and did not get glitchy, at least for me, as I have read that this was not the case for other reviewers.

With this review I did not mean to diminish the work put into this, it works great, and it is entertaining for a while, but one must measure at first feel, and this was not what I expected it to be.
