Dragon VR by Anvio is a short and fun experience about dragons...

So we have to take care of a dragon egg. That means we have to make sure that the egg is not too cold and, above all, that no predators get too close to it.

To do this, we have to complete a few small tasks. First of all, we need fire so that the egg can get a good fire under its shell, i.e. we need to smash together flint, light a torch, distribute firewood, and so on.
However, a curious T-Rex and a cheeky dinosaur bird disturb our little hatchery. But with a cunning trick each we quickly get rid of them.
For our good and selfless work, we are personally taken to a very special place by the dragon mother to initiate the birth of the little dragon with a very simple riddle.

The riddles or tasks are all very simple and logically comprehensible. In addition, the procedures are displayed with huge icons directly above the objects. This is really very simple and therefore Dragon VR is not a game, but really just a little dragon experience.

The controls work amazingly well with the index controllers if you download the appropriate bindings. Takes 2 seconds and goes on the fly. Otherwise only owners of the Vive controllers could control Dragon VR. We grip with the handle and use the left stick to move freely in the area adapted to our own playing surface.

The graphics are surprisingly good. There are small clipping errors and sometimes textures are loaded very late, but on the whole Dragon VR looks very good.

The realistic environment and the creepy critters make me doubt whether the game is suitable for small children, but the puzzle requirement is very low, from there a family fun.
