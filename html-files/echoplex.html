In my relatively short amount of time writing reviews, I haven't seldom see any games with cutscenes, much less cutscenes in games that aren't funded by big publishers. ECHOPLEX breaks out of the mold of most indie games and brings a truly unique experience.

Graphics
ECHOPLEX has two different sides; on one side is the actual graphics in the gameplay. They're minimalstic and fair, nothing hard on the eyes and leaves most to the imagination. The levels are bland, however the action is fast paced and doesn't give you time to really realise the lack of colour in the facility you're traversing (more on that in the Storyline section of the review). The few colours you see are all vibrant neon lights, similar to those in TRON. The other side of ECHOPLEX would be it's general aesthetic (or A E S T H E T I C for the cool kids). The game has a sort-of-retro, sort-of-not type feel with very vaporwave-y effects. Overall the graphics aren't over the top and are minimalstic but the aesthetic of the game ties it all together.

Audio & Soundtrack
The soundtrack in ECHOPLEX was decent to say the least. There was nothing too amazing about it, but that didn't really hinder my experience in any way. Most of the audio you hear is ambiant noises meant to further the aesthetic that really helps build the tone, almost in a horror-sort of way (described as cyber-horror in the description of the game, a description I definitely agree with). I mentioned earlier, however in between levels are these amazingly well done cutscenes that feature both full motion video (using real people as actors and the such) as well as good quality voice acting.

Gameplay
ECHOPLEX's gameplay was decent and definitely not what I'm used to. ECHOPLEX is a puzzle game with the unique element of an Echo, a direct duplicate of yourself several seconds behind you. If you get too close to your Echo, you die and the level restarts. However, you can use your Echo to your advantage by using it to open one way doors, allowing you to traverse levels using your enemy as a key. The puzzles themselves are relatively easy to figure out after a few tries, and all of them require careful timing and "needle-threading" using the movements of yourself and therefore your Echo a few moments after you.

Storyline
The storyline of ECHOPLEX is by far it's strongest point. Like I mentioned in the brief introduction, I rarely see indie games supplied to me with a story and cutscenes as good as the one in ECHOPLEX. The cutscenes are well done FMV (full motion video using actors) that give you tiny little snippets of the story (usually a few seconds at a time with a lot of glitch-y effects, also tying back to the great aesthetic of the game). The story follows the player, an engineer/test subject at CLONOCHEM, a melevolent futuristic corporation. As you play through the levels, you regain your memory bit by bit (shown in cutscenes) and you learn more about your character and the nature of your environment.

Final Thoughts
Overall ECHOPLEX is a decent game with a great story. Although the graphics and gameplay are mediocre at best, the atmosphere, aesthetic and story is defintely a great reason to pick up the game and try it for yourself. If you enjoy games with a good story, puzzle or atmospheric games then I highly recommend you try out ECHOPLEX.
