Follow "First Person Exploration and Puzzle Games" for more games like this!

A PS1-style "horror" exploration game with a unique style, but short on content.

The Good
• The visual aesthetic is fairly unique and works well. It looks similar to Valheim, with a retro pixelation filter thrown on top.

• The audio design is pretty good

• I didn't encounter any bugs in my playthrough, and the performance wasn't bad.

The Bad
• There's not much content - I beat the game in 30 minutes

• There's hardly any gameplay. You go from area to area looking for the thing it wants you to click on. When you click on it, a philosophical audio blurb plays, and a locked door opens somewhere. Then you walk around a bit to figure out which door unlocked, and repeat.

• There's a single, completely trivial puzzle at the end of the game.

• Many of the locked doors never open, leaving the impression that the game isn't finished.

• The story is complete gibberish.

• The game doesn't have many options. There's no way to turn motion blur off - the pixelation filter makes it less noticeable than in most other games, but it's still visible and annoying.

Overall
If you're the sort of person who loves interpreting random nonsense, you'll love this game. For me, this game was a clear "neutral". However since that's not an option, I went with negative simply because of how many "♥♥♥♥" I had.

I feel bad thumbing this down, because it is not a bad game. But it is also not a good one. I love the atmosphere, but that's all this game has going for it. With a better story or gameplay, though, this could easily be a great game. For an example of what that could look like, see Fatum Betula.

Évaluation publiée le 5 février 2023
In the now flooded market of low-poly surreal indie horror, Eschaton does little during its brief runtime to stand out from the swath of other similar titles.
Outside of the visuals and the immersion it briefly crafts, there isn't much to this. An overstated 'binaural' audio experience you can slide between "meditate" and "astral projection" increases the number of warbles or singing bowls you might hear.

The story largely goes nowhere as you simply spend 10-20 minutes walking up to 4 different deities, clicking on them, hearing the abstract musing they have to say in the form of a few sentences and then return to the starting area to piece together one simplistic puzzle which consists of putting a six syllable phrase in order. ...What, you want me to elaborate on more? There isn't any more. That's the entire game.

Even as someone who can enjoy a brief ass walking sim with minimal gameplay I found this to be overpriced with minimal payoff. The visuals and effects look nice, and I wanted to see what was in the next area... but the woefully short experience left me more annoyed than anything.

I find the visual style to be incredible. The best way I can describe it is, imagine what a first-person Golden Sun game would look like — those 3D rendered sprites with rich backgrounds — it's like that, but in a three-dimensional space. Movement feels great.

You can expect a playthrough to clock in at around half an hour or so. I'd love to see this engine revisited and expanded upon. As for the content of the narrative? No ******* clue. Good game.
