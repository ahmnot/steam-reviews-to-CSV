5/10 A 3d puzzle where you switch the direction of gravity.

Good:
- free
- great soundtrack
- interesting core concept of the game to switch gravity in a 3D level and use ledges to reach places

So-so:
- textures used for the walls have high-contrast elements. While these are great for screenshots, in the game they make the level unreadable. The bright baked-in lights in these textures look too similar to actual edges of walls, cubes and other geometry in the level. So, walls feel like they have more geometry to them that there really is. I mean, the puzzle is already difficult, but this visual mess makes it even more difficult, and in a bad way
- camera rotates too slowly, even if you set max rotation speed in options
- English is pretty shaky

Bad:
- at game start you find yourself in a big level which is way too difficult for a new player. Combined with the additional difficulty of simply reading the level's geometry, it ends up just prohibitively difficult. How it should have been instead: the game should have small chambers, like in Portal or Portal 2, where you learn the basics of gravity switching on simple setups. This game does have 1 room with one cube where you switch gravity once to be able to proceed. However, that's not enough for learning.
- in the very first corridor, it's hard to see the narrow passage in the left wall. I simply ran forward and thought the game is bugged. A sign (like e.g. "<") would be helpful. Or maybe the passage should be wider. Or there should just be 1 corridor with 1 left turn.

Overall, the game seems to have an interesting premise. The music is great and the visuals are pretty good. However, the dev probably never playtested it, so onboarding is pretty poor. If you are very patient and like to learn by trial-an-error, this game may be worth a look. But lack of the dev's experience with teaching / introducing new concepts makes it just too unfriendly to the general audience. That's a neutral vote, 5/10.
