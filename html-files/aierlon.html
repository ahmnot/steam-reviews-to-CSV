Évaluation publiée le 7 aout 2022
Aierlon is a first-person platformer that sees you taking part in a series of challenges posed by a small group of gods. Each god has a champion to represent them in these challenges, and you play as the champion of the god, Nuzion. Despite engaging in the same activities, however, you never see the other champions.

The gameplay consists of a mix of platforming and puzzle solving, with the former involving numerous platform hoppings and wall runnings, and the latter encompassing various complex tasks that must be completed in order to progress to the next level. You are given a bow and arrow which can interact with these puzzles as well.

Overall, it's a short game that isn't overly complicated, nor does it become noticeably tedious. The story is entertaining and it features multiple voice actors, which I thought was a nice surprise. The visuals have a distinct retro vibe; I'm not sure if that was the intention but I like it all the same.

My only major complaint is the controls. They are way too slippery for their own good, especially when it comes to the mouse sensitivity, which there is no way to alter whatsoever. This isn't too bad half of the time, but in the more parkour-centric areas, it can become a bit of a problem. Aside from that, I enjoyed my time with Aierlon.

Positives:
+ Reminds me a bit about Montezuma's Return
+ Ok puzzles
+ The running
+ Casual difficulty
+ Ok voiceover
+ Its free

Negatives:
- All the bugs and glitches (needed to start over for the third time to manage to complete it because of this)
- No checkpoint system
- The controls are not optimized enough
- So-so physics
- Unnecessary dialogue (which you must press E for every sentence told)
- Loud footstep sounds
- No adjustments for mouse sensitivity (which is set way too high)
- A lot of low resolution textures
- Very short (over in half an hour max)

If you can manage all the bugs, you might have a good time.
Its got speedrun potential, but the problems make it a lot harder.
But its free and its short, so give it a go if you like Egyptian themed puzzle games.

Total Score: 5/10

PVが結構ネタバレなので注意
一人称視点アクションパズルゲーム。短めながら、割ときちんとした謎解きになっていていいと思う。ただ、ヒントは皆無。アクション難易度は低めなので気軽に遊べるはず。
注意点はコンティニューができないこと。途中でやめた場合は初めから遊ぶしか無い。玉が出てくるが、これを操作できない壁の外側なんかに持っていくと詰むので注意。クリアした感じとしては変なことをしなければバグで進行不可にはならないと思うので、なんとか謎を解くのがオススメ。

気になる点を挙げるなら、なんとなくカメラが実体よりやや前にあるような気がすること。ギリジャンしようとすると落ちるということが結構あった。このへんは好みの問題だと思う。
プレイ中のことでいうと、リスポーン時にどっちから来たか分からなくなること。逆を向いてリスタートしているような時があり、進んでいるはずが戻っている場合があった。あとは音が間違って当てられていたり、音が重なったり、…とはいえプレイにはほぼ影響ない。最後に某ステージの謎判定。ギミックが影響している気はする。

短めながらきちんとしたゲームになっているのでオススメ。好きな人はスピードランしてみてもいいんじゃないだろうか。
