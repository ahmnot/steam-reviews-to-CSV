Shores of Loci is basically a 3D jigsaw puzzle game using completely three dimensional puzzle pieces that naturally fit together (versus the traditional jigsaw puzzle pieces). You'll have to look closely at the puzzle pieces from all sides to find connecting roads, bridges, pillars, etc. in order to connect them to each other. The game currently consists of 4 themed world levels and it took me approximately 2.5 hours to complete the game.

There is a hint system in case you get stuck, but I recommend not using it if at all possible, even if you're stuck. The hint system did not work on the last level, so you will have to just learn to look at the pieces closely. I will say that while I did enjoy the art style and the game in general is beautiful to look at, with flying birds and swimming dolphins in the background, the visuals overall were a bit too low resolution and a bit muddy looking (devs did put this on App Lab for Quest). I also enjoyed the sounds of the game. The game has a soothing soundtrack and nice ambient sounds.

Game is running on the Unity Engine. On my RTX 3080, I was getting steady 90 fps. I did not experience any serious issues or bugs (other than the hint system not working on the last level). You can play this game comfortable seated. You have a force grab mechanic to pick up pieces at a distance. I would have liked to have seen graphical settings available.

I enjoyed the game, but I have to admit, I liked Puzzling Places better. I would recommend both and I hope Puzzling Places comes to Steam soon. I would hope the dev adds more levels to bring better value to the current asking price. I would also request online multiplayer be added in the future and a way to explore the finished puzzle at eye level in first person view as a reward for solving the puzzles.

Rate 7/10.

Such a pretty game! I got a free key to stream it, and I enjoyed it quite a bit. In short: The levels are gorgeous, music is chill, transitions are impressive, puzzles are challenging. There are some things to keep in mind though.

Difficulty
The game feels like a puzzler, but sometimes it can be almost too challenging to see how pieces are supposed to fit together, multiple times I seemingly randomly found the right spot. They have done a good job hiding what would be a seam and what is just any geometry, possibly to a fault. That said, there is a way to prevent frustration, the built in hint system can lead you to the right place.

Interaction
In general interactions are physics based, you can throw pieces around and rotate the bigger part you are building on as if there was no gravity, it's quite satisfying and relaxing. One gotcha is that if you're somewhat tall, moving a piece above your head will make it race away and then move back into your space close to the ground. I think this is a mechanism to return pieces that are out of reach, but I'm actually still holding it, so it's a bit confusing.

Index Controllers
When playing with Index controllers I kept dropping my pieces, it was due to grabbing them with the trigger, then relaxing my other fingers so the grip let go, which would happen even while the trigger was depressed. I simply unbound the grip in the SteamVR settings and that was that, but it was quite frustrating until I realized that was the issue.

Conclusion
If you like relaxed puzzlers with beautiful and chill scenery, this could be for you. Keep in mind the few caveats mentioned though, might take some tweaking to make it play better.

At first, I was skeptical of the $14.99 price tag on this game. I bought it anyway, because the idea of solving 3D jigsaw puzzles in VR was intriguing. And looking back at it after completing the game in just over one hour, I'm still shaken by that price tag.

This game is probably poor value if you look at it objectively. But ultimate I feel like I got my money's worth because Shores of Loci was a new and unique experience for me. The game offers more than just a shattered photograph or painting you need to put back together.

I recommend Shores of Loci to any VR gamers who are also fans of jigsaw puzzling. And I hope the developer makes good on his promise at the end of the game, "Many more worlds will come to the shores."
