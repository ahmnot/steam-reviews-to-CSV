*full disclosure. I helped to beta test this game. I was not paid for my time I don't have any friends or family on the dev team I just answered an ad on Reddit and played the game for a couple weeks.*

I really REALLY like this game. I am an avid guitar games fan and am always looking for something to scratch that itch. But this was on a completely different level. When I got through a tutorial and jumped into my first song it managed to bring back that feeling I was actually a rock star and not some dope with a bunch of overpriced plastic strapped to them. Something over a decade and a half of reality managed to almost crush.

Controls are for the most part rather intuitive lots of feedback to everything your doing so you never really feel like your just waving your hands around. Few of the smaller mechanics can do with some added tutorializing. Satchels funny to have but he's too cool for giving the basics. The devs have added some pop ups to help with the confusion and they are always happy to take feedback on their Discord as to anything that could make the experience better, as well as song suggestions. They're a pretty welcoming bunch.

I've seen a couple people posting reviews complaining about the hand tracking system of the Quest version. Not sure why, maybe they are dissatisfied with how the Oculus storefront handles reviews. I have not played the Quest version, heard mixed things about the way they use camera tracking in your hands. However, it's important to stress the PCVR version is a completely different beast in how it plays. Hand tracking on the Index is really good I'm at the point in my development that I can easily Platinum and Diamond a good chunk of the setlist and the ones I can't usually feels like it's cause of my own incompetence and not the controls holding me back.

Speaking of the set list. It is wall to wall bangers the whole way up. With the exact kind of care and attention put into the progression as a classic GH game. Some easily recognizable stuff to sucker in casual players, ramping up to some lesser known classics and indie bands for the street cred before going all out with stuff like Rush and Skynard in the end.

If you're a guitar games Stan you need to at least give Unplugged a try. I guarantee you're going to find something about this game that you'll love.
