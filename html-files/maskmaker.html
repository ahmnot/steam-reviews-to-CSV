In Maskmaker, you find yourself wandering into the beautiful store of a master craftsman of magical masks. And you’ll soon discover the workshop itself behind the storefront, and it’s there that you’ll learn how to create your own masks with all the tools in the shop.

But of course these are magical masks, and putting them on will transport you to many fantasy worlds. But in order to progress further in each world you need to create new masks, because each mask can only explore a certain area of each realm.

When you see a new mask that you need, take out your telescope to view the mask and then you’ll get a blueprint for how to create it in the shop. And very often you’ll need to search for new components to create these masks. This experience is all about searching and taking in the story that’s narrated for you.

The workshop itself is really fun to use, and so the masks are also enjoyable to create.
There are 3 large realms of masks and with a handy lever you can quickly store and load each of the 3 sets of masks. With that set open, you can also view a minimap to get a better sense of where to go, along with a tally of all the components to be found there.

Later on in the game you’ll face a lot of puzzles, and eventually you’ll need to switch between multiple mask people to change places and solve very large puzzles.

I honestly can’t think of a negative thing to say about this game. It’s so well put together and the VR interactions in the workshop are top notch. I found it incredibly immersive and I loved exploring all these magical realms. Once I started playing, I couldn’t put it down until I finished it, which took me about 5 hours. If you enjoy exploring new magical places, then I think you’ll have an amazing time.

For movement there’s both sliding and teleporting, with comfort options as well.

In Maskmaker erlernen wir die Herstellung mystischer Masken (getestet mit der Valve Index)

Wir schlüpfen in die Rolle eines Lehrlings der durch Zufall über den leerstehenden Laden eines Maskenmachers stolpert. Die Werkstatt selbst dient als Ausgangspunkt für unsere Abenteuer, hier erstellen wir die Masken. Dazu bearbeiten wir einen Holzblock, färben und bemalen die daraus entstandene Maske und behängen sie mit Fundstücken aus den fremden Welten, so entsteht nach und nach eine beeindruckende Sammlung.
Während wir zu Beginn noch einfache Masken erstellen und und damit beschäftigen nach und nach mehr Materialien zu beschaffen müssen wir im späteren Verlauf komplexere Aufgaben erfüllen. Wir reisen nämlich nicht nur in andere Welten, wir übernehmen auch die Kontrolle diverser "Körper" in der Welt, indem wir deren Masken nachbilden. So entstehen komplexe Abläufe in denen wir "mit uns selbst" zusammenarbeiten, indem wir nach und nach mehrere Figuren zum Lösen von Rätseln steuern müssen.

Die Grafik des Spiels ist durchaus in Ordnung wenn man den Comiclook mag, allerdings können wir nur mit Gegenständen agieren die wir auch benötigen, der Rest der Welt ist nur Deko. Die Welt könnte auch ein wenig "lebendiger" sein, aber wenigstens ist alles schön bunt ;)

Zur Steuerung haben wir die Wahl zwischen Teleport und "freier Bewegung" per Thumbstick (in Kopf- oder Controllerrichtung) oder auch eine Mischung aus beidem. Rennen können wir per Druck auf den Thumbstick. Insgesamt stehen uns viele Einstellungsmöglichkeiten zur Verfügung um das Spiel unseren Anforderungen anzupassen, allerdings ist drehen per Stick sehr langsam, dagegen sprinten wir vergleichsweise schnell, da sollte noch ein wenig an der Feinjustierung gearbeitet werden,
Index-Controller funktionieren, allerdings ohne Fingertracking. Als Gesten gibt es nur Hand öffnen oder schließen.

Die Arbeit in der Werkstatt funktioniert klasse, die Masken herstellen und bemalen geht gut von der Hand, die Gerätschaften wirken durchdacht und es gibt eine Menge zu tun. Die Aufgaben in der Welt werden nach und nach komplexer und schwieriger, allerdings zieht sich der Start ein wenig. Bis auf ein paar Kleinigkeiten ist sind mir keine größeren Fehler aufgefallen, selbst die deutschen Untertitel sind gut verständlich.
(Ein Bekannter von mir ist allerdings durch die Welt geglitched)
Wir können aber jederzeit zwischenspeichern, vielleicht sollte man für so einen Fall vorsorglich einen zweiten Speicherstand anlegen...

Insgesamt macht das Spiel auf mich einen ganz guten Eindruck, die Story ist interessant und gut erzählt, die Grafik geht in Ordnung und es ist mal was anderes abseits der Shooter. Ein paar Abzüge gibt es aufgrund der mangelnden Interaktionsmöglichkeiten. Nach nun gut 4 Stunden habe ich bei weitem noch nicht alles gesehen, ich schätze die Gesamtzeit auf ca. 7-8 Stunden ein, da kann man zu dem Preis nicht meckern.

Von daher gibt es von mir einen Daumen rauf, alleine schon für die kreative Idee.

I quite like this! Maskmaker it a pretty fun story puzzle/adventure where you create masks (based on a design) with paint and the items you collect in the world which allow you to take control of the mannequin that wears the same mask in the Maskmaker's world. The basic gameplay is: create a mask and use the mask to go to the Maskmaker's world, collect new items to create new masks and find other mannequins to unlock new mask designs, all while a story unfolds as the king and elders talk to you throughout. There is also a puzzle in each area that you have to work out, usually requiring swapping between multiple masks.

The game is split into 6 areas, where you unlock the first three early on and the last 3 are extensions of the first that you unlock at around the halfway point. I'm pretty sure the first 3 areas had items collected in one area that was need for masks in the other areas (so you might collect the pearl in the beach, and it would be used for a mask in the mountains area). but I thought it was strange that the last three areas do not do that. For that segment of the game, you can pick an area and play through it to the end.

The mask making mechanic is pretty cool. The first time you create the base model of the mask you have to chisel at a wooden block (of course you just hit it and it creates the design). Then you have to dunk it in paint and add items to the snap points around the mask (once you collect one, you have infinite in your workshop).

The second half of the game also tries to be more complex as it requires you to mix colours and introduces paint brushes that allow paint 'sections' of the mask (it's not free form painting). I like the added complexity, but do I wish it went further with it as few masks you make near the end are still pretty basic.

It's not very long (I beat it in 3.5 hours), but I quite enjoy these story adventure games at around this length. And the mask making mechanic it's pretty cool in VR. It's the type of game that probably could work in non-VR, but I think VR adds a lot more fun to what might seem fairly mundane in non-VR.

Additional comments :

Graphics - It is not Crysis, but for VR, it is good enough. At least for me. There were a lot of small details everywhere, so it felt like you are in a place where someone lives. Or lived . Multiple biomes make it exciting everytime where you created new type of mask :)

Gameplay - Game is build around VR, you use your hands to craft the masks, which feels good, much better than using mouse or controller to do it. Very good execution here. Interaction with the worlds is done by hands also. Very good use of VR in this game :).

Audio - there were some ambient audio, but nothing special... nothing that would throw you away. It was better in some places and less noticeable on other places. It was ok.

Audience - There is some story that could be maybe hard to understand by kids. Also the mask making could be frustrating for kids because it is not explained in details, and kids would need guidance if they would be even interested.

PC Requirements - I though I have good enough PC with RTX 2080 would be good enough to run this without any problems. I experienced some frame drops in some areas. Fortunately, I am used to VR so I was not as bad that I would need to get out of VR.

Difficulty - Walking simulator where you need to find items and solve some easy puzzles. Enjoy the story, don't expect much difficulty here :)

Grind - You have some items that you need for the masks, those are mandatory, and then you have some optional collectables - you can ignore those (if you wish) - those are for some story details.
It could be sometimes frustrating for you to find items for masks, but I didn't struggle that much.

Story - this is where the game shines, this is why I wanted to play this game. For it's story ad mask making. It delivered what it should.

Game Time - I have 10 hours on record for this game. But if you won't wonder around like me you can beat it within 5 hours.

Price - Like... it is good game ... But I don't want to play more than 1€ for 1 hour of game play. So 50 % discount would be acceptable for me.

Bugs - I had 2 types of bugs. Items were stuck into textures in various ways. And sometimes dialog does not match with subtitles or, some dialog phrases were skipped. Fortunately nothing major, and if it would be something major, they tent to repeat important messages so it was not a problem.
And for problems with the stuck items. Most of the times if you lost an item it would appear on the original place where you found it the first time. So if you can wait a minute and you know where it was it is good enough.

9 / 10 - very good game meant for VR :). I felt little bit mini-Inception in this game. Because VR googles are like the masks you are crafting in the game. When you put it on, you are transport to the different realm. Bravo to creators for creating masterpiece :)

Overall, I enjoyed the game. The premise was interesting and it was a nice way to really try out VR. I suffer from chronic pain and wanted to see if VR would help take my mind off of it. This game certainly did help with that.

There was a bit of jank though. The controls screen was basically useless, as the text on the controls was so large I couldn't read anything. The subtitles on a few of the sound clicks errored out and just displayed the file name, and part of the ending audio skipped and repeated. I also really struggles with the even at the end of the game, as it was frustrating at too fast. Another concern I had was turning. If I used the angle turning, I was always facing to the side, so I ended up sitting almost sideways on my couch as I played. But if I turned on free turning, there was nothing between "barely turns at all" and "spinning to the point of nausea. It would have been very helpful to have something in between or smaller turn increments so that I could focus on the game and not be distracted by never facing the right way.

I also found some of the puzzles very difficult. Or rather, I'd figure out a puzzle, but then have no idea what to do next. I repeatedly had to follow a playthrough on YouTube to figure things out. I know that there were hints given throughout play, but the hints took way to long to get to the point or were absent all together (like somehow knowing to change to a different mask when it wasn't telegraphed properly).

I liked the creative mode at the end, as well as the ending itself, as I had wanted to play around more with the masks and design my own. If there is a followup or some kind of expansion, it would be cool if putting on your custom masks took you somewhere, maybe to a simple generated world or to revisit the worlds from the game in a new way.

Overall, an enjoyable game. There were some struggles, some of which related to accessibility, but I'm glad I bought the game and got to play through it. If there is a followup or something of the sort, I'd look forward to playing through it as well.

A very captivating, relaxed and chill light puzzle game by the studio that made Fisherman's Tale.

The story is pretty interesting, is superbly voice-acted and accompanied by a beautiful soundtrack. The gameplay revolves around finding 'puppets' in a level, taking note on what mask it has on, then finding the necessary crafting components to recreate it. Putting the mask on also puts you in control of said puppet, allowing you proceed to explore a level further, repeating the process until you eventually reach your goal for the level in question. All the while the story is being delivered to you by the narrator (or rather several of them, all voiced by the same talented actor).

Later on you will be able to swap between four different masks, and use them to control four puppets to solve puzzles. The formula remains basically the same throughout: collect items in a level to use in mask creation, find new mask designs, return to the hub area (your workshop) to craft new masks, put them on, explore further, then repeat. There are a few exceptions here and there, but generally this is what one can expect in terms of gameplay. There are a bunch of collectable items scattered throughout the game. Those things are missable and will require an additional playthough to collect all of them if you miss some on your first run. Provided you care about achievements that is. These collectables are worthwhile to seek out regardless, as picking one up unlocks some additional lore about the world and characters. You won't feel confused about the plot if you miss any of them though, so it's up to you.

Overall, the gameplay loop is built upon a neat little idea that works well despite it's simplicity. The actual crafting mechanics are realized pretty well - from carving to painting and decorating - and are very satisfying.

In terms of VR accessibility, the game can be played both standing and seated, with all the movement options one might expect, such as smooth locomotion, teleporting, smooth or snap turning with adjustable angles. There's also a vignette, an ability to press or toggle the grip buttons to hold things, etc. All in all, very solid and accessible for people with various levels of familiarity with VR. Oh, and you can also save the game at any point to take a break without losing any progress.

Speaking of loss of progress though, I must note that the game crashed several times during my playthrough. Sometimes with a 'UE4 Fatal Error' message, and sometimes just booting me to the desktop without any error messages whatsoever. This wasn't a regular occurence, so whether or not something like this might happen to you is anyone's guess.

The game took me about 4 hours to complete. This being a puzzle game however, your playtime may vary.

Perhaps one of the puzzle games that best exploit the VR potential.

The game is about finding materials to craft new types of masks. Each mask can teleport you to a specific realm or in a specific location therein. Physically removing the mask will bring you back to your workshop, where you can see which materials you still lack, where to find them, build new masks. No shooting, nor gore is present, it's a game about exploration, crafting and puzzle solving.

A lot of care went into the masks creation, which is very "physical" and involves colouring and freely decorating several types of masks. The puzzles are simple and clever at the same time, and well use the masks system. The regions are very unique, each one with its own backstory and peculiarities. The graphic is both charming and gentle on your hardware.

The story is rather simple, but well narrated and coupled to a somewhat rich lore gives a good background to the game.
With an overall playtime of around 5-7h the game doesn't overstay its welcome, and once you start it, it's unlikely you'll put it back to your backlog unfinished.

Slightly longer review:

Each new mask unlocks at least one new component, as well as one to a few new masks. Throughout your explorations you will be joined by a number of voices as a story is revealed explaining not only the history, but also the future of you, the new apprentice, and the Maskmaker's shop.
