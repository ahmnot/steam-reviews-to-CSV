I quite like this! Maskmaker it a pretty fun story puzzle/adventure where you create masks (based on a design) with paint and the items you collect in the world which allow you to take control of the mannequin that wears the same mask in the Maskmaker's world. The basic gameplay is: create a mask and use the mask to go to the Maskmaker's world, collect new items to create new masks and find other mannequins to unlock new mask designs, all while a story unfolds as the king and elders talk to you throughout. There is also a puzzle in each area that you have to work out, usually requiring swapping between multiple masks.

The game is split into 6 areas, where you unlock the first three early on and the last 3 are extensions of the first that you unlock at around the halfway point. I'm pretty sure the first 3 areas had items collected in one area that was need for masks in the other areas (so you might collect the pearl in the beach, and it would be used for a mask in the mountains area). but I thought it was strange that the last three areas do not do that. For that segment of the game, you can pick an area and play through it to the end.

The mask making mechanic is pretty cool. The first time you create the base model of the mask you have to chisel at a wooden block (of course you just hit it and it creates the design). Then you have to dunk it in paint and add items to the snap points around the mask (once you collect one, you have infinite in your workshop).

The second half of the game also tries to be more complex as it requires you to mix colours and introduces paint brushes that allow paint 'sections' of the mask (it's not free form painting). I like the added complexity, but do I wish it went further with it as few masks you make near the end are still pretty basic.

It's not very long (I beat it in 3.5 hours), but I quite enjoy these story adventure games at around this length. And the mask making mechanic it's pretty cool in VR. It's the type of game that probably could work in non-VR, but I think VR adds a lot more fun to what might seem fairly mundane in non-VR.

You won't feel confused about the plot if you miss any of them though, so it's up to you.
