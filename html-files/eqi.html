Pros
- Simple gameplay. Rotate the environment around with left mouse and right mouse, jump from platform to platform, the gameplay is easy to get into while changing up enough to stay fun.
- Nice visuals. The strange, trippy and almost otherworldly look of the game gives it a unique style.
- Great soundtrack. The techno tracks on offer fit the game's theme and provide some good background to the gameplay.

♥♥♥♥
- Some puzzles are tedious. Some stages require crazy levels of precision to finish, which leads to some frustrating trial and error, this is at its worst in the last stage when you can't die unless you go out of bounds, so you don't get a quick restart and have to walk back to the start and do everything over to finish.
- Checkpoints aren't saved if you quit the game. Most stages have checkpoints half way through them so that if you die you don't have to redo everything from the beginning, but if you quit and continue later on you won't start back at the latest checkpoint, you start at the beginning of the stage you were on.
- Controls can sometimes be unresponsive. For some reason the game wouldn't let me jump sometimes when I was running up slopes, which meant I had to stop, wait a second or two, then make a jump, instead of doing everything smoothly.
- No rebindable keys. You have to stick with the default controls, so if you want to anything other than WASD for movement you're out of luck, arrow keys will take you forward and back but instead of left and right strafing they turn the camera.

EQI is a fun little puzzle game with a few flaws, it's tedious at times but it's free, so at worst you'll have wasted an hour or two if you didn't like it, the first half is very enjoyable, the second half is where the cracks start to show, but in general its a solid game worth having a go at that I think most people will enjoy.

I have not completed the game yet, but I do feel like I have experienced enough of it to write a recommendation.

EQI is not a perfect game, but for the price of free, you really have no reason not to try it.

Pros
+ The visuals are great. It is clear a lot of time went into perfecting them. I do feel like the neon theme gets a bit overused, although the later parts of the game do change up the visual style quite a bit.
+ The way the game teaches you its mechanics are completely hands-off. Although a bit frustrating at first, figuring out the solutions to some of the more abstract puzzles or platforming challenges is quite satisfying
+ The music. Ohh boy the music is something. It fits the atmosphere of the game perfectly.

♥♥♥♥
- The game just isn't immersive, as sad as that is. I felt almost no connection with the player character at all, due to there being no footsteps, or environmental sound effects. The only way to interact with the environment is through the characters abilities, which make a very unsatisfying whoosh sound effect. Overall, I wish SOMETHING was told to me about who I am, what I am doing, or how i got into the world of EQI (although maybe this happens at the end of the game)
- Some of the later levels are a bit frustrating. They take some trial and error, which is normal for a puzzle/platformer style game, but everytime you make a mistake you have to wait for 5 whole seconds until you can fall into the void and respawn. The last level amplifies this by not having a void, meaning you have to spend almost 30 seconds walking back over to where you started from.
- The jump just does not work, and needs a lot of tweaking. Going back to the aforementioned way that the player character cannot interact with the environment, zero impact is felt when landing on the ground, making it very hard to determine when I am in the air, which can lead to what feels like missed inputs. It may sound like I'm being a bit harsh or nit-picky, but I would really like to see the game improve. If I had to give a few minor ways I would like to see the game get better, I would recommend

- A little more leeway on when the jump button is pressed, and/or increasing the height of the jump (although I realize that second point may not be viable with the levels the game currently offers.
