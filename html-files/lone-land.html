As many have pointed out already, Lone Land looks very similar to Refunct and appears to have a very similar goal. I had hoped it would play like Refunct as well. Sadly, it doesn't. For me, Refunct was a very enjoyable experience, whereas Lone Land was just an exercise in frustration.

Pro:
+ Good visuals
+ Good music

♥♥♥:
- Very poor control
- You never know if a jump will actually get you where you want to go because you rarely jump high enough and you never know whether or not you will "grab" a ledge
- Sometimes passing near a crystal will pull you to it, pulling you off of a building, and other times it doesn't
- You are penalized (points subtracted at the end of the game) for each time you fall into the water, but many of the crystals require you to fall into the water because there is no building below them
- Most of the time, you need to jump onto a crystal from above very precisely; this is a problem because in order to get the crystals, you need to jump from very tall buildings, and when you miss the crystal (which you will, many times), you have to get to the top of that tall building again, which requires you to go hopping along many buildings each time (it feels like this was intended to waste time, to artificially pad the length of the game)
- There is at least one crystal which is nearly impossible to find (or impossible, depending on how long you want to spend looking for it)
- Sometimes, grabbing a crystal will stop your movement; other times, you will continue to move, frequently propelling you off of the building that materialized when you grabbed the crystal
- Sometimes landing on an overhang behaves normally (your movement stops when you release the movement keys); other times, it behaves as if your feet are greased and you slide right off of it

I'm sure there are other issues, but if you've made it this far into this review and you still aren't convinced that this game isn't worth it, then never will convince you.

To be fair, I read the negative reviews before buying this game, but I thought it looked good (visually) and it looked so similar to ♥♥♥♥♥♥,, so I figured it couldn't be that bad. I was wrong. It can be that bad, and in my opinion, it is that bad. The biggest issue is the control. If movement control and jumping/landing were improved and reliable, then this would be a positive recommendation. Unfortunately, movement control is poor and jumping/landing is not reliable, so I have to say the game is not recommended.

一款综合了跑酷元素的简单的小游戏。
一共100个发光的晶体，触碰到便会在附近刷新出一幢楼房。通过楼房的借力可以到达更高的地方。
（但反正我就只拿到91个剩下9个实在是找不到了）
这一点的设定和Refunct很类似。但Lone Land的跳跃高度与平移距离都比普通游戏大了不少，这也导致了在初步游玩时难以做到精确的定点与位移，稍有影响整体流畅感。

一条突兀的铁轨，一段孤独的旅程，一面高举的旗帜，一个平凡的故事。
我注意到场景之中有这些文字：
“I don't feel past of this place"（我无法逃离）
"I need to find the way out"（我得想办法出去）
"To find the way out I need to overcome my fear"（我得战胜我的恐惧）
"Loneliness is a great place to find yourself"（孤独之中你便可找到你自己）
"Sometimes you have to get up on your own and move on"（也许有时你应该自己起身，然后行动起来）
"I want to travel"（我想去旅行）
"Now I know where to go"（我明晰了我的方向）

用看似无休止的，无规律的，无意义的跑跳与寻找，讲述了一个在孤独中迷失，沉沦，继而静心，奋起，走向远方的故事。
本来应该是像艺术品一样优美，而实际上却有点不尽人意。
玩家的操作和这种孤独的氛围，说实话，没什么代入感。但凡是个单机游戏玩家，都该对这种操作模式烂熟于心，在哪不是个单机，这又谈何孤独？更何况本游戏对氛围的塑造并不出彩。所谓孤独，他到底想表达孤独的什么，是孤独的压抑，孤独的空虚，还是身处于孤独中的人的自在与自得其乐？只可惜在我看来，不论是哪一层含义都没能表达到位。BGM的搭配全程都平庸不扬，若真是像字幕所说那样进行了一个心境的转变，那我想BGM少说也变个调会更有感染力吧。
难以引起共鸣的一场旅程。整体来说游戏内容中等偏下。

Jumping game concept.

This is a memorable game for me. At heart, it's a concept, and priced as such: you start out in a watery void with a railway line reminiscent of Spirited Away, and some glowing lights. If you reach one, it'll spring up a building or two, and more glowing lights (usually). You can jump quite high (moon gravity), so you can jump on the lower rooftops, from there onto the higher rooftops, etc., and you miss a jump, start again from the bottom. Which can be a long way down, eventually. After about an hour, I stopped when my jumping skills weren't up to it any more: the mid-air jumps are a bit frustrating on a 2D display with no depth perception, but they're very rewarding when you get them, sometimes a building pops up right under your feet. But I wasn't able to land some of the higher-up jumps onto buildings too often and got frustrated.

However, the atmosphere and the surreality of houses popping up made the time well spent. I think there's only the one level, but I didn't beat it, so I don't really know.

Recommended if you like making buildings pop up and jumping on them, peacefully!

P.S.: "Refunct" has been mentioned in some other reviews, and both games are similar in that you can make "platforms" pop up by collecting items, and don't have much gameplay beyond the collecting and jumping. Differences:
- Jumps are much higher and more floaty and longer in Lone Land than in Refunct.
- Lone Land pops up way more houses.
- Lone Land usually has several switches active.
- Lone Land creates a progression in how it pops up buildings, it shows you the way to reach the high places by the sequence.
- Lone Land creates a different atmosphere, imbued with more meaning
- Lone Land has actual architecture, not just blocks
- Refunct has more mechanics you need to master: elevators, tubes, wall jumping, ... Lone Land limits itself to jumps and trampolines (and hitting mid-air targets), it's more "pure" in that respect.
- Refunct has an indicator of where you have been.
- I enjoyed Lone Lands more, and think it may be slightly easier and more rewarding while you play it. Lone Lands does a better job of rewarding my progress and giving me a better sense of what comes next.
- That said, if you hated Refunct, you won't like Lone Lands! They're the same genre.
