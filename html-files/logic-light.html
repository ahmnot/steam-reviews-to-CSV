This game was reviewed using gameplay from its launch day version. Your experience on future versions may vary.

Short Answer:
A relaxing and fun logic-based puzzle game. Inventive puzzles at a well-balanced difficulty, with a nice soundtrack to keep you in a good mood throughout. With 80 levels and around 2 hours of gameplay, it's a great time for fans of the logic puzzle genre.

Long Answer:
Logic Light is a logic-based puzzle game in which you need to use an array of power lines and electrical tools to divert electricity back to buildings after a blackout. It starts out simple enough, but slowly increases in difficulty by introducing new objects, new building energy requirements, and putting extra items in your inventory to throw you off. The puzzles are pretty fun, and the difficulty curve goes up at a comfortable pace, making sure you're familiar with multiple iterations of each mechanic before introducing a new one. On a related note, the game has no written tutorials, opting instead to teach you purely through its level design. This approach works well for the most part, but you'll need to be open to experimentation in order to figure out the uses/limits of certain objects that are less obvious at a glance. Once you figure it out, though, it makes for a satisfying experience watching as every house in a level lights up all at once. Adding to the satisfaction is a relaxing lofi soundtrack that accompanies the puzzles; and I highly recommend spending the extra dollar to grab it along with the game, especially if you're a fan of lofi or similar styles of music.

There are 80 total levels in the game, and it took me about an hour and a half to beat them all. It's not the longest puzzle game out there, but it's one that's well worth its low price. Between its balanced difficulty, chill music, and just plain fun gameplay, Logic Light makes for a relaxing time and an easy recommendation to those that enjoy logic puzzle games. The team at Hardcoffee Game Studio have shown that they have a knack for good puzzle design, and hopefully it won't be too long before we get to see more of their game design talent in the future.

Je joue sur Linux. Version Linux : excellente.

Le jeu

Un casse-tête où il faut relier des sources d'énergies à des maisons, afin de les alimenter en électricité. Notre panoplie d'outils est très limitée au début, et évolue un peu au fur et à mesure. Un très léger bémol sur le manque complet de tuto : si la plupart des outils sont intuitifs quand ils sont introduits vu le nombre d'option limitées du niveau en question, ce n'est pas le cas pour tous, et l'un deux n'était vraiment pas clair du tout.

La difficulté augmente progressivement, mais le jeu reste assez facile même sur les derniers niveaux. Ne vous attendez pas à rester bloqué sur un niveau quel qu'il soit.

Graphiquement, c'est plutôt laid, et tout semble mal assorti. Ce n'est pas bloquant, mais on sent bien le manque de polish. La musique lofi est plutôt cool, rien à redire.

Rejouabilité, achievements

Le jeu fait partie de ces casses-têtes qui donnent des achievements avec toutes les lettres de l'alphabet au fur et à mesure des niveaux... rien de spécial à faire si ce n'est compléter tous les niveaux.

En bref

Un casse-tête chill et sans prétentions, peut-être un peu facile.

GNU/Linux

Je n'ai rencontré aucun problème.

Hardware : 5950x, AMD RX 6900XT, 64GB ram.
Screens : Dualscreen, 1x 4K & 1x 1920x1200, un seul screen X.

允许大约 2 小时的游戏以低廉的价格进行愉快的逻辑谜题。
