Escapeworld Dilemma is a solid puzzle, open-world exploration game. It is basically an 'escape the world' by visiting all locations and solving problems related to colors, numbers, music, light refraction, and pattern extensions. There is also a limited amount of collection and crafting required (gathering ingredients for a series of manufacturing processes) and then running those processes. All in all, it is a beautifully crafted and diverse environment with a medium level of challenge. It was developed by just a couple of guys which is pretty amazing.

The gist of the story is that you have been teleported to Escapeworld (without your permission) and your job is to get back home. The journey is a bit complicated and took me 20+ hours of game time to complete. More of my time was spent finding new areas and working through the manufacturing processes than getting stuck on puzzles. The sound track is primarily environmental effects but I got myself into trouble playing with the sound off when I hit a musical clue!

I would recommend it to those who like to explore and solve puzzles with one caveat. This is an early access game. It feels complete but there are still quite a few kinks that the development team is working out. I only encountered one bug in a puzzle solution (which I was able to brute force) and there is one puzzle (quarry) that I am not convinced can be solved with the information in the game. I solved this by contacting another player who had gotten the solution from the developer. Other than that, you will be wise to save in multiple slots and save often. I encountered some glitches related to buttons not working, doors closing, engine freezes, etc. Most all could be resolved by exiting and reloading my last save file. Since I tend to take a lot of notes in this type of game, this was slightly irritating but not overwhelming to me. I might be inclined to wait for the game to go on sale, if I had it all to do over.

I will note that the developer is exceptionally responsive and is actively working with players to resolve issues that are encountered. He responds to forum questions or email and has been very pleasant to work with.

I especially enjoyed the exploration - there are different islands that you teleport or ride a tram to go between. There are unique areas with some fun details if you are paying attention. There is a drone that you can fly and use to move objects between islands. Cruising at a high elevation gives you a great way to get the lay of the land before setting off on foot. Controls are easy (standard WASD and mouse) and all in all, it was a very pleasant game experience.
