war... war never changes. so our painter protagonist chooses painting colorful pictures as escapism, since there's a noticable lack of video games in the 1940s. one night a random stranger shows up at his house and offers him to actually escape into his paintings, the caveat being he has to be back by midnight or get stuck there forever. why he'd want to come back to reality in the first place is beyond me, but hey.

it's your typical linear 1st-person puzzler. walk around, solve puzzles, move on, and put the narrative together yourself, as there's nothing concrete after the intro, until the outro (both voiced). locations are varied, weird and imaginative, but I didn't enjoy any of the puzzles except the escherian castle. too much tedious back and forth in most places, especially in new prague (half my playtime was spent on that one puzzle), plus the usual piano, rotating, etc. mechanics. and way too much stair-climbing. the game could've easily and accurately been called doors and stairs.

tedium aside, the first major problem is the actual time limit of 3 or 6 real-life hours, mentioned nowhere on the store page. why would you want to take your time, explore and look at things for more than a second in such an amazing-looking game, right? sure, there's a war, but the whole point of your journey is to escape from reality, not to get back to it asap. as I found out, explorer mode without a timer unlocks after finishing the game, which is complete nonsense. don't make me waste hours to rush through everything as fast as I can to finally let me play how I wanted to in the first place.

you can shave some time off by replaying earlier chapters faster, because we all know that's what story-based games are all about: speedrunning, repetition and optimization during the first (and for many, the only) playthrough. the time limit is pretty generous, 6 hours is more than enough to finish it, 3 might do it as well (it took me a bit less than that with some outside help), but then why have a timer in the first place? and even if you finish with hours to spare, there's only 15 minutes for the final section, making the clock an even more baffling and unnecessary inclusion.

the second problem is no mid-chapter saving. have to do each section in one go, and all the walking necessary in some of them makes you feel you're wasting too much of already limited time. checkpoints aren't placed at the beginning of new chapters either, so continuing next time you'll have to open a door again, watch the same unskippable cutscenes again, etc. checkpoint-only saving can be done right, but it's not done right here.

either of these things could result in an instant thumbs down, no matter how absolutely gorgeous the game looks (gameplay and functionality come first, eyecandy not necessarily even second), but both of them combined is just a waste and disrespect of the player's time.

it's especially sad because the dev is clearly dedicated and the technical aspects (not just the graphics and the audio) are done really well for the most part, and it runs fine on my pretty low-end hardware, but in the process it forgets that it's just a video game to be played according to one's own schedule, not one dictated by the game.

movement speed is fast enough while sprinting (can be toggled, no need to hold, nor a need to turn it off) and controls do their job but they're not rebindable. you can use a controller, or wasd or arrows for movement, 'e' to interact, mouse to look around, right click to zoom out, tab to bring up the dreaded pocket watch.

various languages, windowed mode, resolution options, graphics quality, motion blur toggle, and a single volume slider are available from the main menu, complemented by an fov slider, invert x/y camera, and even a paint effect slider while playing. audio is mixed properly, so the only slider is not a huge deal, but toggles for sound, music and speech would still be nice. company logos on startup can't be skipped either, and once new game is clicked, there's no way to go back to the main menu after the intro, have to choose a game mode and start playing first.

even though I didn't particularly enjoy the ride, if explorer mode was available from the start and manual saving (or more checkpoints) got patched in, I might cautiously recommend it, the price would be reasonable as well. as it is, I can only suggest watching a let's play on youtube, so you can stop and continue without losing progress, though honestly, you're not missing much by not experiencing it. hopefully, their next game is gonna offer more than pretty things to look at in a well-optimized package.

Ever dance with the devil in the pale moonlight?



Take control of an artist who is offered a deal to escape the madness of war and live for 6 hours inside his paintings. He has to navigate his creations and reach the end before midnight or be trapped forever.



FULL DISCLAIMER: I was provided a free Steam key by the developer ahead of launch but I had already completed this game by receiving it in the May Humble Choice first. So I won’t be labelling this review as ‘Got this game for free’.



THE PROS



    + Vincent would be proud. The low poly look of the game reminds me of The Witness. The way the wind blows through the grass is beautiful and overall the art design is one of the game’s greatest wins. Things look real but only quite real enough to be in a painting. If you want to really go all in on the painterly graphics you can use the slider to max it out and really take a stroll through a van Gogh.



    + The hills are alive with the sound of madness. The music of the game and the accompanying sounds have really been a top priority. I have always maintained how music and ambient sound can make or break a game experience and here the aural experience works really well.



    + paint me a puzzle. Overall the puzzles in the game are a dash of everything. There is some exploration to do, levers to pull and things to rotate. But there are also some really hard order of operation puzzles and thinking way out of the box to do too. There is no real theme to the puzzles (like for instance in Portal or The Witness) and its rather like a mashup of a bunch of different types of puzzles.




    + pace. I don’t think I have ever listed ‘pace’ as a pro or ♥♥♥ before. Let me explain. Summertime Madness is a game that might take you 10 or 20 hours to 100% if you try and find all the secrets by yourself. But it is a game you can quite comfortably complete in about 3 hours or less. Once you do that it might become a game that you can complete in 1 hour. Good news is there is a cheevo for that. There are many ways to delve into madness. It is up to you. You can tackle the game at your own pace and then ramp it up as you see fit.



    + price. For an indie title of this quality I would have expected to pay a bit more than 10 bucks. I am a cheap gamer but this is a very fair price.



THE ♥♥♥♥



    - hint? What hint? The hint system is well intentioned but often falls short of utility. By the time you are ready to take a hint, the hint you are given is usually 3 steps behind what you have already done. There are some points where the hint works out in your favour but in general they are quite vague and there is only 1 hint per level.



THE CONCLUSION



I was very surprised to find Summertime Madness as part of Humble Bundle’s DRM free choice for the month. A whole month before the Steam launch too. The Steam version definitely has all the usual benefits such as cheevos and the ability to take screenshots though.



If you like indie puzzle games then Summertime Madness will give you a good few hours of entertainment and you will take some excellent screenshots along the way.



Pick it up and support your indie devs.

Story:
When a young talented painter’s city comes under heavy attack of a war he tries to focus on his painting. Out of nowhere a stranger suddenly shows up with a particular odd deal. A deal to visit the world within his painting, to escape from the horrors happening outside.
But there is a catch, if the painter doesn’t make it back out of the painting before midnight, he’ll be trapped in there forever. And so his journey begins, as he accepts the deal.
Before and after the painter's journey there is voice over while during the journey there has been made room for interpretive storytelling.

Gameplay:
Right from the moment you dip your toe in the world of the painting the controls get explained to you. While keeping an eye out for several different collectables and even a few secrets, the gameplay with a very few other puzzles, like playing a piano and making sure cogs are in the right order, is mostly about running around and flipping switches in a maze/labyrinth like setting. Which in all fairness sometimes can be very frustrating instead of fun and challenging and it will test your patience.
There are different modes you can play. Explorer with no time limit, classic with a six hour time limit, advanced for a three hour time limit. Beating one of these will unlock expert mode where you have one hour to complete the game. You’re also provided with a hint option in the form of a pocket watch for when you find yourself stuck. Do note that this will add time to your run.

Visuals:
A stunning eye pleaser. It really feels like you get submerged into a painting. Loved every bit of it.
There is also an option under video while being in game to adjust the paint effect to your liking.

Sound:
Adored the sound, it was so peaceful and relaxing that it even faded away in the back of my mind while running around like a lunatic trying to find which lever opens what where who.

Controls:
I played with my controller and everything felt very smooth and easy. Only one small thing: the hitboxes felt very small. I briefly tested it and it seems keyboard mouse is good to go too.

Issues:
I don’t recall seeing a warning for flashing, at some point there was lightning, I’m not photosensitive but the lightning was even harsh on me. So be aware of that if you’re sensitive to it.
There is a certain part where you have to swap between day and night, this makes it so that paths, levers and gates are in a different space. My poor brain does not have the capacity to remember all of that and I’ve spent so much time running around like a headless chicken trying to figure it out.
During a part of the game you will encounter a very sharp horrible sound, which is part of a puzzle, but lil ol’sensitive me really was not happy with it.
The song summertime madness gets stuck in your head of the title. Can’t just be me right?

Closing word:
It’s games like these that make me wonder why steam doesn’t have a rate button between thumbs up and down since the game isn’t bad but also not super wow omg so good. I feel myself torn with mixed feelings to make a choice between those two thumbs.
There were moments I felt frustrated and closed the game only to boot it back up a little later to continue. A true love hate relationship. Yet in the end I did press a thumb up, barely tho. For the little things in the game, the visual beauty, the beautiful music and the story we see at the very end. A story that lingered for a while. Perhaps I’m a little biassed being an art lover. Every time I saw a little reference or a wink to a painter like van Gogh, Dali, Margriet etc, I smiled a little. I found some collectables and I think a secret even and also missed a lot of collectable stuff and perhaps more secrets. I enjoyed the scenery and even took time to throw some rocks in the water, fooling around a bit here and there, catching fireflies not knowing if it’s even an achievement or not. Cause why not? I was playing on explorer since I prefer more relaxing non timed games.
And I have to be honest to myself and say I did not regret this journey. That despite some frustrations, knowing the puzzles a bit better now I am willing to jump back in to go collect those collectables perhaps even try the timed challenges.
If you pick it up and experience a little bit of motion sickness then you can adjust the field of view in settings, this helped me to get rid of that icky feeling.

解谜部分
游戏中所有的关卡都被装在最开始的风景画中，各个谜题也是逐步解锁，谜题虽说不算特别难，但也足够劝退很多方向感和空间感不太好的玩家，游戏内置的看时钟给提示的设计，个人感觉用处不大，本身谜面还是很清楚地，更多考验的是空间解谜能力，即使给了提示，实际效果也不大！个人觉得比较难的是车站的关卡，目标很明确，打开两扇门的开关，找过去的路就可以了，但游戏中用了夜晚和白天切换模式的形式，部分开关甚至路径只在特定的模式下进行，这就需要去记忆位置了，幸好游戏中的开关大多只需要拉动一次就可以了，如果涉及到来回切换开关的话，那复杂程度又将极大提升。另一个需要脑洞的是痛苦那关，进入到该关卡靠近目标会有越来越强烈的杂音干扰，直到昏倒，如此反复并没有可以逃课的办法，唯一的解法竟然是关闭游戏设置中的音量，这个设计既合理又打破了次元壁，实属天马行空，当然过了这里之后音量就会自动恢复！其他的谜题都不难完成，整体游戏解谜难度中等，有些挑战！至于时间的限制大多不用担心，经典模式6小时、高级模式3小时、以及通关后解锁的专业模式1小时，还有不限时间的自由模式可进行选择，时间肯定是够的，提示看的意义不大，看了也一样不知道，还不如去看下视频！

Évaluation publiée le 20 juin 2021
If you’re a fan of first-person puzzle games like Myst, The Witness and The Talos Principle, Summertime Madness is an indie gem requiring your attention. Set in 1945, you play as a painter who makes a mysterious pact to escape his war-torn country and enter one of his paintings. But there’s a catch, if the painter isn’t able to find his way out by midnight, his soul will be forever trapped in the painting.

The game takes inspiration from works of classic literature, such as Christopher Marlowe’s Dr. Faustus, but, perhaps, has more in common with Oscar Wilde’s The Picture of Dorian Gray – another story about a secret pact centred around a painting.

The graphics of the game are stunning. The colours are striking, and the attention to detail impressive, particularly the way the wind blows blades of grass back and forth.

Once in the world of the painting, you land in a hub world of sorts. From here, you complete a series of puzzle dioramas, with the first taking place on a ship. Others include a lighthouse and an urban city. All, in some way, are connected to the hub world, which evolves, and becomes more populated with new things as you complete each puzzle area.

I really liked the way each diorama features a distinctly different aesthetic and has its own unique puzzles. You go from be able to rotate floating platforms in one area to get to a distant door, to finding your way out of an Escher-like maze in another. Some puzzles can be quite tough, and definitely require creative thinking, but there’s nothing fans of the genre should have too much of a problem with. Plus, there is a hint system, allowing you a tip at the expense of time.

And time is important. At the start of the game, there are two game modes: Standard, which gives you 6 hours to complete the game, and Hard, where you have only 3hrs. Clocks are everywhere in the game, reminding you how long you have left. If you fail to escape in the allotted time, you lose, and your soul is trapped in the painting forever.

Both difficulties actually provide you with more time than you need, as the game can be completed in under 90 mins if you know exactly what you are doing. But that’s not going to happen on a first playthrough!

Overall, Summertime Madness is a highly polished first-person puzzler, which features an intriguing story, a memorable world and inventive puzzles.

一个精致的，艺术气息浓厚的超现实主义第一人称解谜小品。

总之，对解谜玩家、步行模拟玩家、风景党、文艺青年，推荐。
